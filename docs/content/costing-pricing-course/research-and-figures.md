## Why this evidence base exists

The founder's brief: teach a small restoration-business owner to work out their **true**
cost of doing business and derive a **realistic, individualised** pricing guide from
**real** numbers — not invented ones. That only works if the reference figures we teach
against are themselves real, current and cited. This file is the single source of truth
for every number that appears in the course modules and the pricing calculator's
default/benchmark values.

Rule for authors: **never** put a number in a lesson or a calculator default that is not
traceable to a row in this file with a source and an "as at" date.

## 1. Superannuation Guarantee (SG)

| Item | Figure | As at | Source |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| SG rate, FY2025–26 | **12.00%** of ordinary time earnings (OTE) | 1 Jul 2025 – 30 Jun 2026 | ATO, "Super guarantee" key rates table |
| SG rate, FY2026–27 | **12.00%** (rate is now at its legislated maximum; no further step-ups scheduled) | 1 Jul 2026 – 30 Jun 2027 | ATO, "Super guarantee" key rates table |
| Maximum contribution base (MCB) | **$62,500 per quarter** of OTE — no SG required on OTE above this | FY2025–26 | ATO, "How much quarterly super to pay" |

**Teaching point:** the 12% is the end of the multi-year step-up schedule (it rose 11.5% → 12%
on 1 July 2025). SG is now stable at 12%, so an owner can plan on it. SG is calculated on OTE
(ordinary hours, most allowances) but **not** on overtime. Worked impact: on a $70,000 OTE wage,
SG costs the employer **$8,400/yr** ($70,000 × 12%). This is a real, unavoidable on-cost on top of
the wage — a common blind spot for owners who "pay themselves what's left".

## 2. Wages / labour — the Modern Award

Restoration technicians in a small AU restoration business are most commonly covered by the
**Cleaning Services Award 2020 [MA000022]** (the award that covers building/industrial cleaning
and restoration-type cleaning work). Some structural/reinstatement work may instead fall under
the **Building and Construction General On-site Award [MA000020]** — the course must tell owners
to confirm their actual award/agreement with Fair Work, because coverage drives the legal minimum.
`[UNVERIFIED — accountant/Fair Work to confirm the correct award for the specific business]`

### Cleaning Services Award 2020 [MA000022] — minimum rates from first full pay period on/after 1 July 2025

| Classification | Min weekly (full-time) | Min ordinary hourly | Saturday | Sunday | Casual ordinary (+25%) |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Cleaning Services Employee Level 1 | $982.20 | **$25.85** | $38.78 | $51.70 | $32.31 |
| Cleaning Services Employee Level 2 | $1,014.70 | **$26.70** | $40.05 | $53.40 | $33.38 |
| Cleaning Services Employee Level 3 | $1,068.40 | **$28.12** | $42.18 | $56.24 | $35.15 |

Source: Fair Work Ombudsman Pay Guide — Cleaning Services Award MA000022 (rates effective from
first full pay period on/after 1 July 2025); FWO award page awards.fairwork.gov.au/MA000022.

Loadings under the award:
- **Casual loading: 25%** on the minimum hourly rate (cl 11.2).
- **Part-time loading: 15%** on the minimum hourly rate for each ordinary hour (cl 10.2).
- **Annual leave loading: 17.5%** on 4 weeks annual leave (standard modern-award/Fair Work Act
  s90 entitlement). Source: Fair Work Act 2009 s90; FairWorkMate hiring-cost tool notes.

**Teaching point:** the award hourly rate is the *floor*, not the real cost, and not what most
restoration techs are actually paid (skilled restoration labour typically sits above award). The
course teaches the owner to start from *their actual pay rate* and build the on-cost stack on top.

**Rule-of-thumb on-cost multipliers** (used by AU trade charge-out calculators, as a sanity check
only — the course teaches building it from the business's real numbers, not the rule of thumb):
- 20% = lean (super + insurance only)
- 25% = typical (super, leave loading, insurance, workers comp)
- 30% = safe/full (adds payroll tax, uniforms, training, admin)

Source: Lucrature Charge-Out Rate Calculator (AU, 2026); Better Back Office True Labour Cost
Calculator (2026) — both state the real cost is "40–70% higher" than the bare wage once super,
workcover, leave, public holidays and non-productive time are included.

**Worked cross-check (FairWork Mate Hiring Cost TCO tool, verified 2026-05-01):** on an $80,000
base wage → total annual cost to employ **$96,076.92** (≈20.1% overhead), comprising SG @12%
$9,600 + leave loading $1,076.92 + training @5% $4,000 + recruitment amortised $1,000, with
payroll tax **$0** (business under the state threshold). Effective cost ~$48.62/hr on a 38h × 52w
basis — **before** adjusting for non-billable time. Source: fairworkmate.com.au/tools/hiring-cost-tco.

## 4. Workers compensation — premium rates (state-based, industry-classified)

Workers comp is **compulsory**, priced as a percentage of payroll, and the rate depends on both
the **state** and the **industry classification** the business falls under. Restoration work can be
classified as cleaning services *or* building/construction services depending on the scope — the
two attract very different rates. This is a major teaching point (and a real cost lever).

Published 2025–26 industry rates (percentage of wages):

| State (scheme) | Relevant classification | Rate 2025–26 | Source |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| NSW (icare) | Cleaning Services (non-Govt) WIC 786610/786620 | **5.76%** | icare NSW WIC rates 2025-26 PDF |
| NSW (icare) | Carpet Cleaners 952130 | 6.21% | icare NSW WIC rates 2025-26 |
| NSW (icare) | Building Exterior Cleaning & Maintenance 425930 | 9.69% | icare NSW WIC rates 2025-26 |
| NSW (icare) | Construction Services n.e.c. 425940 | 6.69% | icare NSW WIC rates 2025-26 |
| WA (WorkCover WA) | Building & Other Industrial Cleaning 73110 | **2.53%** | WorkCover WA Recommended Premium Rates 2025-26 |
| WA (WorkCover WA) | Painting & Decorating 32440 | 4.53% | WorkCover WA 2025-26 |
| ACT | Building & Other Industrial Cleaning Services 7311 | ~4.53–4.79% | ACT Suggested Reasonable Premium Rates 2025-26 |

**Teaching point:** the *same* type of work costs **2.53% of payroll in WA but 5.76% in NSW** —
more than double. The course must show the owner how to find *their* WIC/industry rate from *their*
state scheme (icare NSW, WorkSafe VIC, WorkCover QLD, RTWSA, WorkCover WA, etc.) rather than guess.

owner to (a) know their state threshold, (b) confirm they're under it, and (c) plan for when growth
crosses it — because it's a step-change cost that can surprise a scaling business. Grouping rules can

**Teaching point:** with CPI at 4.0% and interest rates elevated (cash rate 4.35%, biased higher), an
owner who doesn't re-price at least annually is **going backwards in real terms** — costs (wages, fuel,
insurance, equipment) are all rising faster than a flat price list. The course frames annual re-pricing
as non-optional and ties it to the CPI/award-increase cycle (award rates rise each 1 July). It also
uses the interest-rate context to justify pricing in a cash-flow/working-capital buffer, since insurance
work is often paid in arrears.
