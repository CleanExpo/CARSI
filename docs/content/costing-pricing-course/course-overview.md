## The market gap this fills

Most restoration-business pricing courses teach you to *copy the market* — look at what the
competitor down the road charges, match it, maybe shave 10% to win work. That is how good
operators go broke while staying busy. It ignores the one thing that actually determines whether a
job makes money: **your** costs, which are different from everyone else's.

There is no course in the Australian restoration market that takes a small-business owner by the
hand through **their own numbers** — award-driven labour with all its on-costs, state-specific
workers comp and payroll tax, real insurance premiums, equipment payback, GST/PAYG set-asides,
and the non-billable hours that quietly eat a technician's day — and turns those into a **costed,
defensible, individualised pricing guide**. That is this course.

The output is not a generic rate card. It is *the student's own* break-even number, chargeable-hour
rate, and job-pricing model, built from figures pulled from their own bookkeeping and signed off by
their own accountant.

## Course description

**Building Your Real Pricing Guide** takes a restoration-business owner from "I guess my prices"
to "I have a costed pricing guide I can defend to a client, an insurer, and my accountant."

You start by understanding *why* cost-based pricing beats copying competitors, then build your true
cost picture piece by piece: fixed vs variable costs, the full overhead stack, and — the part almost
everyone gets wrong — the real, fully-loaded cost of an employee once you add super, leave loading,
workers comp, payroll tax and the hours they're paid for but can't bill. From there you layer in
insurance and risk, tax and super set-asides, then calculate your break-even and your true
chargeable-hour rate. Finally you assemble the pricing guide itself — labour, equipment, materials
and margin — and take a one-page summary worksheet to your accountant to sign off before you rely on
a single number.

Every figure in the course is real, current (July 2026), and cited to its source. Every calculation
uses **your** numbers, not made-up examples — the built-in pricing calculator pulls your actual
profit-and-loss and expense categories straight from your cloud bookkeeping (Xero, QuickBooks or
MYOB), or you enter them manually or by CSV. The result is a pricing guide that is individual to your
business, your state, your award, your equipment and your overheads.

This is educational content, not financial or tax advice. The course is built around an explicit
**accountant-verification gate**: you finish by producing a summary your registered accountant or tax
agent confirms before you use it.

## Who this is for

- **Owner-operators** of small restoration businesses (water, fire, mould) who set their own prices.
- **New restoration businesses** pricing their services for the first time.
- **Established operators** who suspect they're undercharging but can't prove it.
- **Estimators / office managers** who need to understand the cost basis behind the quotes they write.

Prerequisites: none. You need access to your own business numbers (or your bookkeeping login) and,
ideally, a relationship with an accountant or bookkeeper. No accounting background is assumed.

## What you'll be able to do (learning outcomes)

On completion, the student will be able to:

1. Explain why cost-based pricing produces sustainable margins where competitor-copying doesn't.
2. Separate their business's costs into fixed and variable, and account for the full overhead picture.
3. Calculate the **real, fully-loaded cost of an employee** — award/actual wage + super + leave
   loading + workers comp + payroll tax + non-billable time — and express it as a cost per *billable*
   hour using a realistic utilisation rate.
4. Quantify insurance costs and build an explicit **risk loading** for excess exposure.
5. Set aside the right amount for **GST, PAYG and income/company tax** so BAS and tax time aren't cash
   shocks.
6. Calculate their **break-even point** and their **true chargeable-hour rate**.
7. Assemble a complete **pricing guide** covering labour, equipment, materials and target margin.
8. Populate the whole model from their **real numbers** — imported from cloud bookkeeping, or entered
   manually / by CSV.
9. Produce an accountant-ready summary worksheet and understand exactly what to ask their accountant
   to verify before relying on it.

## The accountant-verification gate (non-negotiable design feature)

Because this is paid financial-education content that produces numbers an owner will use to price real
jobs, the course is built around an explicit verification step, not a disclaimer buried in the footer:

1. **Module 9 requires** the student to generate a one-page **Accountant Verification Worksheet**
   (auto-produced by the calculator) that lists every input, every rate used and its source/as-at date,
   and the resulting break-even, chargeable-hour rate and target margin.
2. The student is instructed to take that worksheet to a **registered tax agent or accountant** and
   have them confirm: (a) the award/wage basis, (b) the state workers-comp and payroll-tax treatment,
   (c) the tax/super set-aside assumptions, (d) depreciation/asset treatment, and (e) that the target
   margin is realistic for their structure.
3. The course completion state records that the student *has been prompted* to verify — the platform
   does not certify the numbers themselves.
