## Module 1 Quiz — Introduction to Scoping

**10 questions | Pass mark: 70%**

### Q1. What is the primary purpose of a scope of works in a restoration project?

A) To calculate the technician's hourly rate
B) To define the full extent of work required, including materials, labour and equipment
C) To satisfy the building owner's curiosity about the damage

### Q2. Under IICRC S500, who is ultimately responsible for determining the scope of a water damage restoration project?

A) The insurance loss adjuster
B) The property owner
C) The qualified restoration professional on site
D) The plumber who identified the water source

A) AS/NZS 3500.1
B) AS/NZS 4349.1
C) AS/NZS 1170.2
D) AS/NZS 3000

**Correct: B**

### Q4. An estimator's scope of works should include all of the following EXCEPT:

A) Line-item labour and material quantities
B) Photographic evidence of affected areas
C) The property owner's personal insurance policy details
D) Equipment requirements and placement strategy

**Correct: C**

### Q5. What does the acronym "IICRC" stand for?

A) International Institute of Cleaning and Restoration Certification
B) Institute of Insurance Claims and Restoration Compliance
C) International Interior Cleaning and Renovation Council
D) Industry Institute for Certified Restoration Contractors

**Correct: A**

### Q6. Why is it critical to document pre-existing damage during the initial site inspection?

B) It protects the restorer from liability for damage that occurred before the loss event
C) It is only necessary for commercial properties

A) They are the same document with different names
B) The scope defines what work is required; the quote assigns a price to that work

D) Only insurance jobs require both; private jobs need only a quote

A) The policyholder directly, with no insurer involvement
B) The insurer or their appointed loss adjuster/building consultant
C) The local council building inspector

### Q9. A well-prepared scope of works reduces the risk of which common project problem?

A) Technician absenteeism
B) Scope creep and cost overruns
C) Weather-related delays
D) Subcontractor licensing issues

**Correct: B**

### Q10. Under IICRC S500, what is the restorer's first priority upon arrival at a water damage site?

A) Begin removing wet materials immediately
B) Contact the insurance company for authorisation

## Module 3 Quiz — Water Damage Scoping

**10 questions | Pass mark: 70%**

### Q1. Under IICRC S500, Category 1 water is defined as:

A) Water containing significant contamination including bacteria and sewage
B) Water originating from a sanitary clean source with no substantial risk from dermal, ingestion or inhalation exposure
C) Grey water containing chemical or biological contaminants
D) Any water that has been standing for more than 48 hours

**Correct: B**

### Q2. A toilet overflow containing urine but no faecal matter is classified as which category under IICRC S500?

A) Category 1
B) Category 2
C) Category 3
D) It depends on the property type

**Correct: B**

### Q3. Class 2 water damage is characterised by:

A) Minimal water absorption; only a small area is affected
B) Significant water absorption into porous materials; wet walls up to 600 mm and wet carpet/underlay
C) Water has saturated virtually all materials in the room including ceilings
D) Only hard, non-porous surfaces are affected

**Correct: B**

### Q4. Which of the following materials would typically be classified as non-restorable after exposure to Category 3 water?

A) Sealed tile flooring
B) Stainless steel benchtop
C) Carpet underlay (foam-based)
D) Glass windows

**Correct: C**

### Q5. When writing a scope for a Class 3 water damage, you should account for:

A) Drying of surface water only — walls and subfloor are unaffected in Class 3
B) Water wicking into walls, ceilings from above or below, and full saturation of at least one wall cavity
C) Complete demolition of all building materials in the property
D) Only the carpet and underlay in the affected room

**Correct: B**

### Q6. The primary drying goal under IICRC S500 is to:

A) Remove all visible standing water and then allow natural evaporation
B) Restore materials to their pre-loss dry standard within an acceptable timeframe to prevent secondary damage
C) Achieve a moisture content of exactly 0% in all materials
D) Run equipment for a minimum of 14 days regardless of readings

**Correct: B**

### Q7. How many air movers per dehumidifier is the general starting ratio for a standard drying setup?

A) 1:1
B) 2:1
C) 4:1 (approximately 4 air movers per dehumidifier)
D) 10:1

**Correct: C**

### Q8. An affected materials inventory should include:

A) Only items that are visibly wet to the naked eye
B) All materials within the affected zone including those that test above the dry standard, noting type, quantity and restorability
C) Only materials the property owner identifies as damaged
D) A list of replacement products and their retail prices

**Correct: B**

### Q9. Category 1 water can escalate to Category 2 or 3 if:

A) The property owner requests it
B) The water remains untreated for an extended period, allowing microbial growth or contact with contaminants
C) A loss adjuster inspects the site
D) Category escalation only occurs with fire damage

**Correct: B**

### Q10. When scoping a water damage job, "containment" refers to:

A) Packing the property owner's contents into boxes
B) Isolating the affected area to prevent cross-contamination and control the drying environment
C) Placing warning tape around the exterior of the building
D) Locking the property so no one can enter during restoration

**Correct: B**

### Q1. Xactimate is primarily used in the Australian restoration industry for:

B) Producing detailed, line-item cost estimates for insurance restoration claims

### Q2. When costing labour for a restoration scope, which of the following should be included?

A) Base hourly rate only
B) Base hourly rate, superannuation, workers' compensation, annual leave loading and on-costs
C) Only the amount the technician takes home after tax
D) A flat daily rate with no breakdown

**Correct: B**

### Q3. The difference between "gross margin" and "markup" is:

A) They are the same calculation expressed differently
B) Gross margin is profit as a percentage of the selling price; markup is profit as a percentage of the cost
C) Markup is always lower than gross margin
D) Gross margin applies only to materials, markup applies only to labour

**Correct: B**

### Q4. A dehumidifier hire rate of $85/day is quoted on a scope. The job runs for 5 days with 2 dehumidifiers. What is the total dehumidifier cost?

A) $425
B) $850
C) $170
D) $680

**Correct: B**

### Q5. "Overhead recovery" in a restoration estimate refers to:

A) The cost of replacing the building's roof
B) Allocating a percentage of the company's fixed operating costs (rent, insurance, vehicles) to each job
C) Charging the client for the estimator's travel time only
D) Adding a contingency for unexpected material price increases

**Correct: B**

### Q6. SimPRO is commonly used by Australian restoration companies for:

A) Producing IICRC certification documents
B) Job management including quoting, scheduling, invoicing and cost tracking
C) Conducting laboratory analysis of mould samples
D) Generating weather forecasts for drying predictions

**Correct: B**

### Q7. When an insurer requests a "breakdown" of your estimate, you should provide:

A) A single lump-sum figure with no detail
B) Separate line items for labour (with hours), materials (with quantities), equipment (with days/rates) and any subcontractor costs
C) Only the profit margin percentage
D) A comparison with three competitor quotes

**Correct: B**

### Q8. Which of the following is a common reason for estimate rejection by a loss adjuster?

A) The estimate uses industry-standard software
B) Line items lack supporting documentation such as measurements, photos or moisture readings
C) The estimate includes GST
D) The restorer holds IICRC certification

**Correct: B**

### Q9. A restoration company targets a 30% gross margin. If the total cost of a job (labour + materials + equipment) is $10,000, what should the selling price be (approximately)?

A) $13,000
B) $14,286
C) $10,300
D) $15,000

**Correct: B** _(Selling price = Cost / (1 - Margin) = $10,000 / 0.70 = $14,286)_

### Q10. "Scope creep" in pricing refers to:

A) A deliberate strategy to increase the estimate after the job starts
B) Uncontrolled expansion of work beyond the original scope, often without corresponding price adjustment
C) The natural increase in material costs due to inflation
D) A technique used by loss adjusters to reduce claim values

**Correct: B**
