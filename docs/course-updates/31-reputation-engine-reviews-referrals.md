## Block 3 · Draft content

### 3a. New section — "A star is worth 13–18% of revenue"
> In Australia, your Google rating isn't vanity — it's a revenue lever you can measure. A **one-star
> improvement** correlates with a **13–18% lift in revenue** for a small business, and **78% of consumers won't
> even contact a business under 3.5 stars**, no matter how close or cheap you are. With **89% of Australians**
> (and **94% of people booking tradies**) reading reviews before they choose, your star rating is the price of
> entry to the conversation. This course builds the system that earns those stars on purpose instead of by luck.

### 3b. New section — "The 2-hour review window"
> Most owners ask for reviews too late, if at all. The timing is everything: ask **within 2 hours** of finishing
> the job and about **42%** of customers leave a review; wait two days and it collapses to **~6%.** The moment of
> maximum goodwill is right after you've solved their problem and they're relieved and grateful — that's when the
> ask lands. Build it into the job close: the invoice goes out, the thank-you goes out, and the review request
> goes with it, same day, every time. A seven-fold difference in response rate comes down to *when* you ask.

### 3c. New section — "Reply or lose them"
> Getting reviews is half the job — **replying** is the half almost everyone skips. Only **34%** of Australian
> businesses respond to their reviews (sole traders just **19%**), yet responders see **2.7× higher
> conversion**, collect 41% more positive reviews over a year, and are more likely to land in the Local Pack.
> Reply to *every* review, positive and negative, in a human voice — a thank-you on the good ones, a calm
> problem-solving reply on the bad ones. Silence tells the next customer you're not paying attention; a thoughtful
> reply tells them exactly the opposite.

### 3d. New section — "Referrals are your cheapest leads"
> The best lead you'll ever get is a referral: in AU trades they convert at **65%+** because the trust is already
> built before the phone rings, and tracked referrals convert **2–4× better than cold leads.** Most experienced
> owners already get referrals — they've just never built a *system* to generate more. Make the ask routine:
> after a great job, "if you know anyone else with water damage, we'd love the introduction." Give past customers
> an easy way to pass your name on. A referral habit running in the background of every job beats a bigger ad
> budget, and it costs nothing.

### 3e. New section — "Retention beats acquisition"
> Winning a new customer costs **5–7 times more** than keeping one you've already served. In a restoration or
> cleaning business that means the maintenance client, the repeat commercial account, the customer who calls *you*
> next time — those are the cheapest revenue you have. The reputation engine ties it together: a great job earns a
> review (asked within 2 hours), the review earns trust that wins the next customer, and a referral ask turns one
> happy customer into two. Fewer than half of Australian small businesses even solicit feedback — so this loop is
> a genuine, underused edge.

### 3f. "Interesting fact" hook
> Two numbers reframe how much money is hiding in your reviews. First: for an Australian small business, **one
> extra star is worth roughly 13–18% more revenue** — a measurable line item, not a feel-good metric. Second: the
> review request has a **two-hour half-life** — ask right after the job and **42%** of customers say yes; wait two
> days and it's **6%.** Most owners have the goodwill and miss the window. Fix the timing and you've quietly built
> a revenue engine your competitors are ignoring — only **34%** of AU businesses even reply to the reviews they get.

## Block 5 · Considered & rejected
1. **Review-gating tactics** (filtering unhappy customers away from public review) — **rejected on ethics and
   policy**: it breaches Google's review policies and Australian consumer-law expectations. The course teaches
   asking *every* genuine customer and replying honestly — never buying, incentivising or gating reviews.
2. **Platform-specific automation walkthroughs** — trimmed to the principle (ask within 2h, reply to all);
   overlaps with course N5 (AI comms) which owns the automation *how-to*. Keep the boundary.
3. **NPS / survey theory** — too corporate for an owner-operator audience; kept the one actionable loop instead.

## Conversion / learning hypothesis (M-2)
- **Metric:** quiz on the 2-hour window, reply-to-all, referral ask, retention economics; self-report of a
  review-ask step added to job close.
- **Target:** ≥50% of finishers add a same-day review request to their job-close routine within 14 days.
- **Kill threshold:** revise if the action rate <25%.
- **Next variant:** if flat, lead with 3a (a star = 13–18% revenue) to anchor the stakes in dollars.
