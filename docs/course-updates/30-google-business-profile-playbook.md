## Block 3 · Draft content

### 3a. New section — "The map pack is the phone book now"
> When a customer in Parramatta types "emergency water damage near me" at 9pm, three businesses appear in the
> Google map pack — and one of them gets the call. If you're not in those three, you don't exist to that
> customer, no matter how good your work is. Your **Google Business Profile** is what decides whether you're in
> the pack. It's free, it takes an afternoon, and for most Australian small businesses it's now **more important
> than your website** for getting found locally. This course is the playbook for owning your three suburbs.

### 3b. New section — "Complete beats clever"
> You don't need marketing genius — you need a *complete* profile, because Google rewards completeness bluntly.
> Complete profiles get around **7× the clicks** of half-finished ones. So finish it: correct business name,
> address and phone (your **NAP**, identical everywhere online), every service listed, service areas set, hours
> right, and both a **primary and secondary category** that match what you actually do. The most common reason a
> good business is invisible isn't competition — it's a profile that was set up once and never finished.

### 3c. New section — "Photos and posts are ranking signals"
> Photos aren't decoration — they're a lever. Profiles with **100+ photos get about 520% more calls** than
> average. And **recency** counts: Google reads photos uploaded in the last 90 days and regular posts as proof
> the business is active. Make it a habit — a few job photos a week (before/after dries out beautifully in
> restoration), a monthly post about a service or a seasonal risk. Ten minutes a week of upkeep outranks a
> competitor who set-and-forgot two years ago.

### 3d. New section — "Reviews feed the pack — and the AI"
> Reviews are the biggest thing you can move. **4.7 stars and 30+ reviews** wins a top-three map-pack spot about
> **60%** of the time, and in Australia a business with 40 reviews beats a zero-review rival almost every time.
> But there's a 2026 twist: your reviews now feed **AI answers** too. When a customer asks ChatGPT or Perplexity
> "who's a good water-damage company in Perth?", the AI synthesises your reviews, photos and profile — and about
> **1 in 7** Australian enquiries already involves an AI assistant. A steady stream of recent reviews is now
> ranking fuel for both Google *and* the AI layer sitting above it. Reply to every review, in a human voice.

### 3e. New section — "The 20-review threshold and a simple cadence"
> Aim for a steady **5–10 new reviews a month** — achievable for almost any local business — until you clear the
> credibility threshold (customers hesitate below ~20 reviews) and settle into the trust zone. The trick isn't a
> big campaign; it's a **habit**: ask every satisfied customer, at the moment the job's done and they're happy.
> Photos in the last 90 days, a post or two a month, a reply to every review, and a handful of fresh reviews —
> that's the entire maintenance routine, and it's what keeps you in the pack while competitors drift out of it.

### 3f. "Interesting fact" hook
> For a local Australian service business in 2026, your **Google Business Profile now matters more than your
> website** for getting found — and it's quietly become your entry ticket to AI too. The same reviews, photos and
> categories that put you in Google's three-business map pack are what ChatGPT, Perplexity and Google's AI
> Overviews read when a customer asks them to recommend someone. About **1 in 7** enquiries already passes through
> an AI assistant — and the businesses it names are the ones with a complete, active, well-reviewed profile.

## Conversion / learning hypothesis (M-2)
- **Metric:** quiz on completeness, photos/recency, the 4.7★/30-review pack threshold; self-report of profile
  actions taken (photos added, categories fixed, review cadence started).
- **Target:** ≥50% of finishers complete a GBP audit + one improvement within 14 days.
- **Kill threshold:** revise if the action rate <25%.
- **Next variant:** if flat, lead with 3f (GBP feeds AI) to make the stakes current.
