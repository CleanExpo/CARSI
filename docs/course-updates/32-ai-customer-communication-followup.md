## Block 3 · Draft content

### 3a. New section — "The follow-up that never gets sent"
> Most owners don't lose customers to competitors — they lose them to **silence.** The quote goes out, the job
> gets busy, and the second and third touch never happen. The cost is enormous: structured multi-touch follow-up
> pulls roughly **90% response versus 9%** for a single message. That's not a small tweak — it's the difference
> between converting the enquiries you already paid for and watching them go cold. The problem was never that you
> don't care; it's that follow-up depends on memory and time, and you're short on both. That's exactly what AI
> fixes.

### 3b. New section — "Let AI draft, you approve"
> The safe, high-value way to use AI in customer communication is a simple division of labour: **AI drafts, you
> approve, it sends.** AI writes the follow-up email, the quote reminder, the review reply — you read it, adjust a
> line, and it goes. Businesses using AI to craft personalised **review responses** see about **23% higher
> satisfaction** because every customer gets a thoughtful, timely reply instead of silence. The guardrail is
> non-negotiable: nothing customer-facing sends unread. AI removes the *effort* of follow-up, not your *judgement*.

### 3c. New section — "Match the channel to the customer"
> More messages isn't the goal — the *right* message on the *right* channel is. Australian consumers lean to
> **email** and social, and they explicitly want **personalisation and the right frequency**, not a firehose.
> Some channels simply get seen: business messaging (WhatsApp-style) opens at **over 90%** versus about 20% for
> email, and a **missed-call auto-SMS** catches the customer you couldn't answer. Use AI to tailor the same
> follow-up to each channel and each customer — a short SMS nudge, a fuller email with the quote — so it reads
> like you wrote it personally, because in the way that matters, you did: you approved it.

### 3d. New section — "AI review replies that sound human"
> Replying to every review is proven to lift conversion — but it's the task owners never get to. AI closes that
> gap: feed it the review and your voice, and it drafts a reply you tidy in ten seconds. Done well, AI-assisted
> responses lift satisfaction **~23%**, because customers see an owner who's present and listening. The trap to
> avoid is the **generic, templated reply** — customers spot it instantly and it backfires. The fix is the same
> guardrail: AI drafts, you add the one specific detail that proves a human read it, then it posts.

### 3e. New section — "Trust is the Australian unlock"
> Here's the twist that makes this course work in Australia: the #1 reason local owners avoid AI is **trust** —
> about **65%** distrust it or want to keep humans in control. Good instinct. The way to use AI *and* honour that
> instinct is the guardrail you've now seen three times: **AI drafts, a human approves, it sends.** The most
> common safeguard among AU businesses already using AI is exactly this — checking outputs before they reach a
> customer. Add one more layer of good practice: be willing to be transparent that you use tools to respond
> quickly. Used this way, AI closes the perception gap (72% of AU SMBs think their digital presence is strong;
> only 49% of customers agree) without ever putting a machine between you and your customer.

### 3f. "Interesting fact" hook
> The single most under-used growth lever in a service business is almost embarrassingly simple: **follow up more
> than once.** Structured multi-touch follow-up pulls around **90% response versus 9%** for a one-and-done
> message — a tenfold difference — yet most quotes get exactly one touch because follow-up runs on memory and
> time. AI removes both constraints. And the very thing that makes Australian owners nervous about AI — **trust,
> the #1 barrier at ~65%** — is solved by the same rule that makes it work: AI drafts, a human always approves
> before it sends.

## Block 5 · Considered & rejected
1. **Fully-autonomous AI agents answering customers unsupervised** — **rejected**: contradicts the AU trust
   reality (65% barrier) and good practice; the course's spine is human-approval. Autonomy without a check is
   exactly what owners (rightly) fear.
2. **Chatbot-build tutorial** — trimmed; tool-specific builds date fast and belong in a tools annex. The course
   teaches the *draft→approve→send* operating model, which survives any tool.
3. **Overlap with N2 (Speed to Lead)** — deliberately bounded: N2 owns the *first* response and the missed-call
   text-back; this course owns the *ongoing* multi-touch follow-up and review/communication engine. Cross-linked,
   not duplicated.

## Conversion / learning hypothesis (M-2)
- **Metric:** quiz on multi-touch vs single-touch, the draft→approve→send guardrail, channel matching; self-report
  of an AI-drafted follow-up or review-reply workflow set up.
- **Target:** ≥40% of finishers stand up an AI-drafted follow-up or review-reply workflow (with human approval) within 30 days.
- **Kill threshold:** revise if the setup rate <20%.
- **Next variant:** if flat, lead with 3a (the follow-up that never gets sent) and add a restoration-specific sequence example.
