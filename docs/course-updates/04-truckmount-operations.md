## Block 3 · Draft content

### 3a. New section — "How a truckmount actually makes heat"
> A truckmount doesn't have a boiler. It runs an engine to drive the vacuum blower and the
> high-pressure pump, and it recycles the *waste heat* from the engine and blower exhaust through a
> heat exchanger to warm your cleaning solution — a good machine holds 230–265°F (110–129°C) at
> normal carpet-cleaning flow. Detergent is injected last, just before the hose, so the pump and
> heat exchanger only ever see water. Understand that path and every maintenance rule below makes sense.

### 3b. New section — "The one rule that voids warranties: water hardness"
> Scale is a truckmount's slow killer, and it comes from hard water. In any hard-water area — roughly
> 3.0 grains per gallon or more — a water softener isn't optional: HydraMaster (and the physics)
> require one, and running without it will **void your warranty**. Periodic descaling on its own
> won't save you; scale on the inside of the heat exchanger quietly steals heating performance until
> the machine underperforms and parts fail. Soften the water going in, and descale on schedule anyway.

### 3c. New section — "Current machines (2026)"
> - **HydraMaster TITAN 575HP (2026):** now ships with 2000 PSI heated pressure-washing as standard —
>   turning the truckmount into a multi-surface plant (carpet, tile, LVP, concrete, exterior washdowns).
>   Stainless multi-stage heat exchanger, Cerakote exhaust to cut radiant heat inside the van, 400+ CFM
>   blower, 32.5HP liquid-cooled Kubota with a 3-year warranty.
> - **Sapphire Scientific 370 EFI (Legend Brands):** 26.5HP Kohler EFI running at ~65% of max for longer
>   engine life, patented Thermal Well fin-tube heat exchanger, and a 5-year limited warranty on the heat
>   exchange, frame and waste tank.
> - The pattern to notice across 2025–26 models: **stainless heat exchangers, Cerakote exhaust coatings,
>   and higher-pressure hard-surface capability** are becoming standard, not upgrades.

### 3d. Operating essentials box
> - **Full throttle only.** Never clean with the engine at idle — you risk serious engine and component damage.
> - **Pre-vacuum, then pre-treat and let it dwell 10–20 minutes.** The solution touches the fibre for
>   only a few seconds; the dwell does the real work and prevents over-wetting and residue.
> - **Protect the blower.** Solids drawn into the blower cause major damage; keep the recovery-tank screens clean.
> - **Descale on a schedule** matched to your water — deposits cut heating performance first, reliability second.

### 3e. "Interesting fact" hook
> Your truckmount heats water with an engine's exhaust — the same heat a car throws away out the
> tailpipe is what puts 250°F into your cleaning wand. Which is exactly why the cheapest thing on the
> job — the water going in — decides how long the most expensive thing on the job lasts.
