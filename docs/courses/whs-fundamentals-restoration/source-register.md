## Disclaimer (must remain prominent)
This course is **educational / pre-training material only**. It is **not** Australian or New Zealand
legal advice or legal representation. It is a best-practice educational summary compiled from published
research and public regulator guidance, current to July 2026. The official legislation, Codes of Practice,
Australian Standards, Safety Data Sheets and regulator directions always take precedence. The loud
"NOT LEGAL ADVICE" banner appears in the course description, a dedicated "read first" notice module, and at
the top of every content module (8 banners) — do not remove it.

## Origin
Rebuilt AU-original from a US OSHA/HAZWOPER source document found in Google Drive
`CARSI Courses/RTO Courses/OH&S/OSHA Hazwopper.docx`. The US framework (OSHA, 29 CFR, HAZWOPER, MSDS, PELs,
N95, GFCI, 120 V) does not apply in Australia and was fully replaced with the AU equivalents below.

## Research method
`/nexus` deep research — three parallel subagents (WHS legislation; hazardous materials/silica; restoration
hazards & AS/NZS standards) — then **Exa Tier-1 corroboration** of every load-bearing, drift-prone claim
against Safe Work Australia, legislation.gov.au and Standards Australia.

## IICRC / CEC terminology compliance
- `cecHours: null`, `iicrcDiscipline: null`. **No IICRC CEC claim is made** — correct, as this is general WHS
  content and CARSI is an IICRC **CEC provider**, not a certifier.
- IICRC S500 / S520 / S540 are cited only as **recognised industry methods** used to meet the AU WHS duty of
  care — never as Australian law and never as "IICRC courses/certification". Passes `check:iicrc-terminology`.
