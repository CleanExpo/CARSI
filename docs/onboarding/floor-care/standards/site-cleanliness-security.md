# Onsite Cleanliness, Security & Confidentiality Standard (Pillar 7)

**Purpose:** Set how we keep our own work area clean and safe, stay security- and child-aware, and protect the client's privacy and our reputation.

---

## Core message

We are a cleaning and maintenance provider. If **our** work area is messy, unsafe or disorganised, we have no credibility. Our own footprint on site must be cleaner and tidier than anyone else's. How we leave a site says more than how we found it.

---

## Clean, organised work zones

**Do:**
- Keep tools, leads, hoses and chemicals in a tidy, contained area while working
- Bag and remove your own rubbish, packaging and used pads as you go
- Wipe up and dry overspray, drips and spills immediately
- Keep walkways, doorways and exits clear at all times

**Not that:**
- Gear sprawled across a corridor
- Empty bottles, wrappers, tape or pad scraps left behind
- Hoses or leads left across a path or doorway unattended
- Wet floor left unsigned

## No mess, no uncontrolled leads or hoses

- Run leads/hoses along walls; cover or ramp where they must cross a path; sign wet areas
- Never leave a lead/hose live and unattended across a walkway
- Coil and stow the moment a zone is done
- Take all waste off site or to the agreed bin — never the client's general bins without permission

## Respect for site property

- Protect walls, doors, lifts, skirting and furniture when moving gear
- Don't move, borrow or use the client's equipment, power, water or storage without permission
- Report any accidental damage immediately and honestly — never hide it
- Leave fixtures and settings (taps, thermostats, doors, alarms) as you found them

## Security awareness

- Follow the site's sign-in/out and access rules every visit — **[COMPANY TO CONFIRM sign-in process]**
- Wear ID; challenge nothing yourself but report anyone or anything that seems out of place to the site contact
- Don't prop open secured doors or share access codes/keys/fobs
- Don't let unknown people "tailgate" you through secured doors
- Lock the vehicle and secure chemicals/tools when unattended
- Lock up / re-arm / hand back keys exactly as instructed when you finish

## Restricted areas

- Go only where the job requires and where you're authorised
- Treat server rooms, offices, records rooms, medication/first-aid areas, staff-only and student areas as off-limits unless the task and the site contact say otherwise
- If you need access to a restricted area, ask — don't let yourself in

## Child-safe conduct

On schools, childcare and youth sites, child safety overrides convenience.

- **Never be alone with a child.** Stay visible and in open/shared areas; if a child approaches, keep it brief, public and refer them to a staff member
- Never take a child anywhere, give them anything, or have private contact (including online/phone)
- Keep your work zone barriered so children can't reach gear, leads or chemicals
- Report any child-safety concern to the site's nominated staff and your supervisor immediately

> **A Working With Children (WWC) check is only a starting point.** Clearance does not replace supervision, visibility, or following the site's child-safe rules. It is necessary, not sufficient.

This standard reflects the **National Principles for Child Safe Organisations** — including operating under a clear child-safe code of conduct. Working with children also carries state/territory WWC requirements — **[COMPANY TO CONFIRM jurisdiction and check requirements]**.

## Confidentiality

- Don't discuss or repeat private site information — names, layouts, security details, incidents, what you saw or overheard — with anyone outside the job
- Don't post about sites, clients or children on social media
- Don't read or remove documents, screens or records
- What happens on a client's site stays on the client's site

## Photography & private information — strict rules

**Do not photograph or record:**
- People or children (in any frame, even background)
- Documents, screens, whiteboards, noticeboards, student work, records or anything bearing private information
- Site security details (alarm panels, access points, keys)

**Only photograph** the floor/area/defect strictly needed for the job — framed to exclude people and private information — and only where the company process allows.

**Grounding (kept brief):** Under the **Privacy Act 1988** and the **Australian Privacy Principles (APPs)**, an image of an identifiable person is **personal information**. Personal information should only be collected where **reasonably necessary** for the work, and used only for that purpose. When in doubt, don't capture it.

## Assume you are always being watched

- **Assume CCTV is operating everywhere on site**, including outside and in corridors
- Behave as though every action is visible to the client — because it usually is
- This isn't about being watched; it's about being the kind of crew that doesn't need watching

## Protecting company reputation

Every visit either builds or burns the company's reputation. Clean work zones, secure conduct, child-safe behaviour and tight confidentiality are what keep clients renewing. One careless photo, one piece of gossip, one unattended chemical can cost a contract.

---

## End-of-job "leave-no-trace" checklist

- [ ] All rubbish, packaging and used pads bagged and removed
- [ ] Spills/overspray cleaned and floor dried
- [ ] Hoses and leads coiled, stowed, nothing left across paths
- [ ] Chemicals removed/secured; nothing left within reach of children or the public
- [ ] Walls, doors, lifts, furniture undamaged; any damage reported
- [ ] Restricted/secured areas closed, re-armed or locked as instructed
- [ ] Wet-floor signage collected once safe and dry
- [ ] No photos of people, children, documents or screens taken or stored
- [ ] Keys/fobs/access handed back; signed out per site process
- [ ] Site contact updated on completion; area left cleaner than found
