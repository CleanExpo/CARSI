# Onboarding Pathways — 30 / 60 / 90 Days

> The journey from new starter to independent operator, in three stages. Each stage has a goal, what
> the new starter does, what the supervisor does, and the gate to the next stage. Timeframes are a
> guide — **competence comes before the calendar.** A new starter who needs longer gets longer.

How the stages relate to the rest of the pack:
- **Modules & content:** `01-module-structure.md`
- **Assessment & sign-off:** `03-assessment-framework.md`, `checklists/supervisor-sign-off.md`
- **On-the-job checklists:** everything under `checklists/`

Stage entry assumes **induction is complete** (`checklists/staff-induction.md`) — paperwork, WWC check
sighted, PPE/uniform issued, the seven standards walked through, and the confidentiality/child-safe
acknowledgement signed — **before** the new starter attends a live site.

---

## Stage 1 — 30-Day Onboarding Pathway: "Safe and supervised"

**Goal:** the new starter is safe, understands the standard, and can perform core tasks **under direct
supervision**, always paired with a competent operator.

**Focus modules:** 1 Introduction · 2 Health & Hygiene · 3 WHS/PPE/SDS · 4 Surface ID · 6 Machinery
operation (intro) · 8 Site setup (intro).

**The new starter:**
- Completes knowledge checks for Modules 1–4.
- Learns the daily pre-start and site-arrival sequence **by doing them every day** with a buddy.
- Selects PPE from the SDS and labels chemicals correctly, every time.
- Identifies common surfaces and the safe/avoid chemistry for each.
- Operates core machines under supervision; never works alone.

**The supervisor:**
- Pairs the new starter with a competent operator on every job.
- Signs off Modules 1–4 (knowledge + practical) on
  `checklists/supervisor-sign-off.md`.
- Watches for the automatic-fail acts and coaches early.

**Gate to Stage 2 (by ~Day 30):**
- [ ] Induction complete and acknowledgements signed.
- [ ] Modules 1–4 passed (Module 3 at the 90% safety standard).
- [ ] Can complete a daily pre-start and site-arrival checklist accurately with prompting.
- [ ] No outstanding safety concerns.

---

## Stage 2 — 60-Day Competency Pathway: "Competent with lighter supervision"

**Goal:** the new starter runs standard jobs **with lighter supervision** — competent on method,
machines, hazard control and conduct, with a supervisor checking rather than guiding each step.

**Focus modules:** 5 Methods · 6 Machinery operation (full) · 7 Vehicle/equipment maintenance · 8 Site
setup & hazard control (full) · 9 Professional conduct.

**The new starter:**
- Completes a full method end to end to standard (Module 5).
- Operates each machine safely with the correct consumables and lead/hose control (Module 6).
- Completes the daily pre-start independently and raises a correct fault report (Module 7).
- Sets up a compliant, signed, hazard-free work zone (Module 8).
- Meets the conduct standard with site contacts (Module 9).
- Communicates delays/risks/issues early.

**The supervisor:**
- Steps back to spot-checking; lets the new starter lead tasks.
- Signs off Modules 5–9 (90% on the safety-critical Modules 6, 7, 8).
- Reviews evidence quality (notes, photos, completed checklists).
- Introduces the disruption matrix (`02-risk-disruption-matrix.md`) — prevention and escalation.

**Gate to Stage 3 (by ~Day 60):**
- [ ] Modules 5–9 passed (safety-critical modules at 90%).
- [ ] Runs a standard job with only spot-check supervision.
- [ ] Pre-start, site-arrival, chemical/PPE and machinery checklists used correctly and unprompted.
- [ ] Conduct and communication meet the standard with no client concerns.

---

## Stage 3 — 90-Day Independent Operator Pathway: "Cleared to work solo"

**Goal:** the new starter passes the final practical assessment and is **cleared to run standard jobs
independently**, with clear escalation paths for anything outside the standard.

**Focus modules:** 10 Confidentiality/Security/CCTV · 11 Job completion & handover · 12 Final practical
assessment.

**The new starter:**
- Applies the confidentiality, security and CCTV rules with no breach (Module 10).
- Closes out jobs to standard with compliant evidence and correct escalation (Module 11).
- Completes the **Module 12 final practical assessment** — a full standard job, end to end, assessed
  against the checklists and standards.

**The supervisor:**
- Conducts the final practical assessment (`03-assessment-framework.md`).
- Confirms the new starter knows **when to stop and escalate** (unexpected floor condition, equipment
  failure, confidentiality conflict, anything outside scope).
- Signs the **independent-operator clearance** below.

**Independent-operator clearance (Day ~90):**
- [ ] Modules 10 & 11 passed (Module 10 at the 90% reputation standard).
- [ ] Module 12 passed: ≥85% overall **and** 100% on all safety- and reputation-critical items.
- [ ] All 12 modules signed off on `checklists/supervisor-sign-off.md`.
- [ ] Escalation paths understood and named.

## After 90 days

Independence is the start of the standard, not the end of development. Set:
- A **refresher / re-validation cycle** for safety-critical modules (3, 6, 7, 8, 10) [COMPANY TO CONFIRM
  interval].
- Pathways into **specialist work** (e.g. strip-and-seal lead, pressure-washing with run-off control,
  supervisor track) [COMPANY TO CONFIRM].
- Ongoing **toolbox talks** drawn from real disruptions and near-misses
  (`05-implementation-recommendations.md`).

> A new starter who needs 45 days for Stage 1, or 120 days to reach independence, is not a failure —
> they are someone being held to the standard. Do not sign the clearance until the work earns it.
