# Training Module Structure — 12 Modules

This is the full training structure. Each module follows the same shape so trainers and new starters
always know what to expect:

- **Learning objective** — what you can do at the end.
- **Why it matters** — the real-world reason.
- **Key lessons** — what is taught.
- **Practical demonstration** — what you show a supervisor, on a real or mock job.
- **Supervisor sign-off** — what the supervisor confirms before you move on.
- **Sample assessment questions** — the kind of questions in the knowledge check.
- **Pass/fail requirement** — the bar to clear.
- **Evidence required** — what is recorded.

Assessment detail and sign-off rules are in `03-assessment-framework.md`. Surface, chemical and
equipment detail is in the `standards/` and `checklists/` files referenced in each module.

## Module 1 — Introduction to Professional Floor Care

- **Learning objective:** Explain what professional floor care is, the difference between cleaning,
  deep cleaning, polishing, sealing and maintenance, and why floors matter in sensitive facilities.
- **Why it matters:** If you understand *why* the job is done a certain way, you make better
  decisions on site instead of just following steps.
- **Key lessons:**
  - What floor surface care covers: routine cleaning, deep/restorative cleaning, polishing/burnishing,
    sealing/coating, and ongoing maintenance.
  - Why floors are high-contact, high-risk surfaces — everyone walks on them, and they carry tracked-in
    soil, spills and contaminants.
  - How poor floor care affects hygiene, presentation, slip safety and the life of the floor (asset).
  - Why it matters more in schools, childcare, public buildings and commercial sites.
  - Careful language: floor care **supports** hygiene and **reduces** soil load — it does not cure or
    sterilise.
- **Practical demonstration:** Walk a site with a supervisor and correctly name the floor types and
  the service each needs.
- **Supervisor sign-off:** New starter can describe the five service types and why floor care matters
  in a sensitive facility, in their own words.
- **Sample assessment questions:**
  - Which of these is *maintenance* rather than *deep cleaning*?
  - Why is a childcare floor treated as a higher-risk surface than a back-of-house storeroom?
- **Pass/fail:** Correctly distinguishes the service types and gives at least three reasons floor care
  matters. **Pass = 80%** on the knowledge check.
- **Evidence required:** Completed knowledge check; supervisor note in the induction record.

## Module 2 — Health, Hygiene and Child-Sensitive Environments

- **Learning objective:** Identify the contaminants floor care helps manage, prevent cross-contamination,
  and adjust your work for child-occupied and sensitive spaces.
- **Why it matters:** Children, staff and vulnerable people use these floors at close range. Doing the
  job in the wrong order, or with dirty equipment, spreads soil instead of removing it.
- **Key lessons:**
  - What we help manage: pollen, dust, dirt, body soils, food soils, spills, tracked-in contaminants,
    and conditions that support mould growth.
  - Why correct process and clean equipment matter — dirty pads, mops and solution re-deposit soil.
  - Cross-contamination prevention: clean-to-dirty work order, colour-coded equipment, separating
    toilet/kitchen tools from general-area tools.
  - Working in child-sensitive environments: low-tox product choice, ventilation, isolating wet areas,
    and timing work around occupancy.
  - Infection-control *awareness* — we support a facility's hygiene; we do not make medical or
    disinfection-outcome claims (see guardrails).
- **Practical demonstration:** Set up colour-coded equipment and explain the clean-to-dirty order for a
  classroom or care room.
- **Supervisor sign-off:** Uses correct work order, keeps equipment separated, and adjusts method for an
  occupied child space.
- **Sample assessment questions:**
  - Why do we clean from clean areas toward dirty areas?
  - Name two things you change when working in an occupied childcare room.
- **Pass/fail:** Demonstrates cross-contamination control and one correct child-space adjustment.
  **Pass = 80%.**
- **Evidence required:** Knowledge check; demonstration sign-off; photo of correct colour-coded setup.
- **Related:** `checklists/chemical-ppe.md`.

## Module 3 — WHS, PPE, SDS and Chemical Safety

- **Learning objective:** Read a Safety Data Sheet, select and wear the right PPE, and store, decant,
  label and handle cleaning chemicals safely.
- **Why it matters:** Our chemicals include strippers and acids/alkalis that can burn skin and eyes and
  give off fumes. The SDS tells you how to use them safely — it is not optional reading.
- **Key lessons:**
  - What a Safety Data Sheet is, the duty to keep current SDS (not more than five years old) and a
    chemicals register accessible to workers.
  - How to read the SDS sections that matter on the job: hazards, PPE, first aid, handling and storage,
    spill response.
  - Selecting PPE *from the SDS* — gloves, eye protection, footwear, respiratory protection where
    required.
  - Correct labelling of decanted/transferred containers; never an unlabelled bottle.
  - Safe storage and segregation on the vehicle and on site; basic spill response.
  - Jurisdiction flag: most states follow the model WHS framework; **Victoria operates under its own
    OHS Act and Regulations** — check the rules for the state you are working in.
- **Practical demonstration:** Find the PPE and first-aid sections on a real SDS, then set up and label a
  decanted chemical correctly.
- **Supervisor sign-off:** Selects correct PPE from an SDS, labels a decanted container, and states the
  spill-response steps.
- **Sample assessment questions:**
  - How current must an SDS be to be considered current under the model WHS framework?
  - You decant a stripper into a spray bottle. What must you do before using it?
- **Pass/fail:** Correct PPE selection and correct labelling, with no unsafe handling. **Pass = 90%**
  (safety-critical module).
- **Evidence required:** Knowledge check; SDS exercise; supervisor sign-off.
- **Related:** `checklists/chemical-ppe.md`, research pack §WHS/SDS.

## Module 4 — Floor Surface Identification

- **Learning objective:** Identify common floor surfaces, tell sealed from unsealed and porous from
  non-porous, and know which chemicals and methods are safe for each.
- **Why it matters:** The fastest way to cause expensive damage is to use the wrong chemical or pad on a
  surface — acid on stone, a stripper on timber, a harsh pad on a soft finish.
- **Key lessons:**
  - Recognising vinyl, VCT, linoleum, rubber, concrete, terrazzo, ceramic/porcelain tile, natural stone,
    timber/engineered, epoxy/resin, and carpet/carpet tiles.
  - Sealed vs unsealed; porous, semi-porous and non-porous, and why it changes dwell time and chemical
    choice.
  - pH awareness: neutral, alkaline and acidic cleaners, plus solvents, sealers, strippers and polishes —
    and where each belongs.
  - How the wrong chemical or method damages a surface, and the "test a small area first" rule.
- **Practical demonstration:** Identify five surfaces on site and state a safe and an unsafe chemical for
  each.
- **Supervisor sign-off:** Correctly identifies common surfaces and matches safe chemistry/method to each.
- **Sample assessment questions:**
  - Why should you never use a strong acidic cleaner on natural stone?
  - How do you tell whether a vinyl floor is sealed?
- **Pass/fail:** Identifies the common surfaces and avoids every damaging combination shown. **Pass = 85%.**
- **Evidence required:** Knowledge check; surface-ID walkaround sign-off.
- **Related:** `standards/floor-surface-identification.md`.

## Module 5 — Cleaning, Polishing, Sealing and Maintenance Methods

- **Learning objective:** Carry out the core methods correctly — routine and deep cleaning, polishing/
  burnishing, stripping and sealing, and post-service maintenance — using dwell, agitation, extraction,
  rinsing and neutralising properly.
- **Why it matters:** Method discipline is what makes the result consistent. Skipping dwell time, rinsing
  or neutralising is what causes streaking, residue, re-soiling and slip problems.
- **Key lessons:**
  - Why dwell time, agitation, extraction, rinsing, neutralising, polishing and sealing each matter.
  - Carpet: hot water extraction vs encapsulation, and the dry-time vs soil-removal trade-off in occupied
    rooms.
  - Hard floors: tile and grout method, and verifying slip resistance after cleaning or sealing.
  - Vinyl strip-and-seal end to end: strip → rinse/neutralise → dry → thin sealer/finish coats.
  - **Cure, re-entry and dilution come from the SDS/TDS, not from guesswork** — and slip-resistance
    targets come from the relevant standard.
  - Maintenance after service so the result lasts and the next visit is easier.
- **Practical demonstration:** Complete one full method end to end (e.g. a tile-and-grout clean or an
  encapsulation pass) to standard.
- **Supervisor sign-off:** Follows the full method with correct dwell, agitation, rinse/neutralise and
  finish, and defers cure/re-entry to the SDS/TDS.
- **Sample assessment questions:**
  - Where do you get the re-entry time for a freshly sealed floor?
  - When would you choose encapsulation over hot water extraction in a school?
- **Pass/fail:** Completes a full method to standard with no step skipped. **Pass = 85%.**
- **Evidence required:** Knowledge check; method demonstration sign-off; before/after photos.
- **Related:** `standards/floor-surface-identification.md`, research pack §carpet/§strip-and-seal.

## Module 6 — Machinery and Tool Operation

- **Learning objective:** Operate the core floor-care machines and tools safely and correctly, and select
  the right pad, brush or tool for the job.
- **Why it matters:** These machines are powerful and run on power or batteries near water and people.
  Wrong pad, wrong setting or careless operation damages floors and injures people.
- **Key lessons:**
  - Rotary machines, auto scrubbers, burnishers, wet vacuums, extractors and pressure systems — what each
    does and when to use it.
  - Pad and brush selection and how it changes the result.
  - Safe operation around water, leads and hoses; electrical safety and RCD use with portable equipment;
    keeping leads and hoses managed as trip hazards.
  - Pre-use checks before a machine touches the floor.
- **Practical demonstration:** Safely set up and operate two different machines with the correct pad/brush
  and lead/hose management.
- **Supervisor sign-off:** Operates the machines safely, picks the correct consumable, and manages leads/
  hoses without creating hazards.
- **Sample assessment questions:**
  - Which pad would you fit for burnishing, and which for aggressive stripping?
  - What do you check on a lead before plugging a machine in?
- **Pass/fail:** Safe operation of each machine with correct consumables; any unsafe act is an automatic
  fail. **Pass = 90%** (safety-critical).
- **Evidence required:** Knowledge check; machine operation sign-off per machine.
- **Related:** `checklists/machinery-maintenance.md`.

## Module 7 — Vehicle, Machine and Equipment Maintenance

- **Learning objective:** Complete the daily pre-start and the weekly/monthly maintenance checks that keep
  the vehicle, TruckMount, machines and equipment ready, and report faults correctly.
- **Why it matters:** A job does not fail only because of poor cleaning. It fails because the vehicle is
  not ready, the TruckMount cannot get to site, a machine will not start, the right tool was left behind,
  PPE or chemical is missing, a battery is flat, or a hose is damaged. **If it is not checked, it can stop
  the job.**
- **Key lessons:**
  - Vehicle presentation and readiness; TruckMount readiness where fitted.
  - Daily pre-start checks across machines, hoses, leads, tools, pads/brushes, PPE, signage, barriers,
    chemical stock, spill kit, first-aid kit and fire extinguisher.
  - Weekly and monthly maintenance tasks and tool inventory.
  - Fault reporting: what to log, who to tell, and when a fault means the equipment is out of service.
  - The backup principle — carry spares for the things that stop a job (pads, fuses, key chemicals).
- **Practical demonstration:** Complete a full daily pre-start using the checklist, and raise a fault
  report on a (simulated) defect.
- **Supervisor sign-off:** Completes the pre-start accurately and raises a correct fault report.
- **Sample assessment questions:**
  - Name three pre-start items that, if missed, can stop a job on arrival.
  - What do you do when a machine fails its pre-start check?
- **Pass/fail:** Completes the daily pre-start with no critical item missed. **Pass = 90%.**
- **Evidence required:** Completed pre-start checklist; fault report; supervisor sign-off.
- **Related:** `checklists/vehicle-equipment-pre-start.md`, `checklists/machinery-maintenance.md`,
  `02-risk-disruption-matrix.md`.

## Module 8 — Site Setup and Hazard Control

- **Learning objective:** Run the on-site sequence from arrival to work-area setup — sign-in, briefing,
  surface inspection, risk assessment, pre-start checks, and controlling the work zone.
- **Why it matters:** Most incidents and complaints come from the setup, not the cleaning — an
  un-signed wet floor, a trailing lead, a blocked exit, or starting before checking the surface.
- **Key lessons:**
  - Arrival and site sign-in; making contact with the site contact; taking the job briefing.
  - Surface inspection and on-the-spot risk assessment before starting.
  - Setting up the work zone: wet-floor signage, barricading/exclusion zones, keeping exits and paths
    clear, and managing leads and hoses.
  - Wet floor and just-sealed floor controls in occupied buildings.
- **Practical demonstration:** Set up a compliant work zone with signage, barricading and managed leads/
  hoses, and talk through the risk assessment.
- **Supervisor sign-off:** Runs the arrival sequence and sets up a controlled, signed, hazard-free work
  zone.
- **Sample assessment questions:**
  - What goes up before you make a floor wet?
  - You need to run a lead across a corridor children use. What do you do?
- **Pass/fail:** Sets up a controlled work zone with correct signage and hazard control. **Pass = 90%.**
- **Evidence required:** Knowledge check; site-setup demonstration sign-off; photo of compliant setup.
- **Related:** `checklists/site-arrival.md`, `standards/site-cleanliness-security.md`.

## Module 9 — Professional Conduct and Client Expectations

- **Learning objective:** Meet the company standard for uniform, personal hygiene, behaviour, language,
  site presentation and communication with the client.
- **Why it matters:** On a school or childcare site, how we behave is as visible as how we clean.
  Professional conduct is what earns the trust that keeps the contract.
- **Key lessons:**
  - Uniform, personal hygiene and presentation.
  - Behaviour and language on site; respect for children, staff, teachers, visitors, tenants and facility
    users.
  - Vehicle cleanliness and where to park; where to unload, where to place chemicals, how to run hoses and
    leads.
  - Controlling the work zone so it does not disrupt site operations.
  - Communicating delays, risks and issues early and clearly.
- **Practical demonstration:** Role-play arriving on a sensitive site — greeting the contact, presenting
  professionally, and communicating a delay.
- **Supervisor sign-off:** Presents to the company standard and communicates professionally with a site
  contact.
- **Sample assessment questions:**
  - A teacher asks you to move because a class is coming through. How do you respond?
  - Where should chemicals be placed on a childcare site, and where should they never be?
- **Pass/fail:** Meets the conduct standard with no behaviour that would concern a client. **Pass = 85%.**
- **Evidence required:** Conduct sign-off against the standard; supervisor observation note.
- **Related:** `standards/professionalism-conduct.md`.

## Module 10 — Confidentiality, Security and CCTV Awareness

- **Learning objective:** Keep the work area secure, respect restricted areas, protect private
  information, and conduct yourself as though CCTV is always running.
- **Why it matters:** We work inside other people's facilities, often around children and private
  information. One photo of a screen or a child, or one careless comment, can breach trust and the law.
- **Key lessons:**
  - Keeping work zones clean, controlled and secure; not blocking or propping secure doors.
  - Restricted areas and child-safe conduct; never being alone with a child.
  - Confidentiality: do not discuss private site information; do not photograph people, children,
    documents, screens or private information.
  - Privacy awareness at a high level (Privacy Act / APPs) — why the photo rule exists.
  - Assume CCTV is operating; behave as though every action is visible to the client; protect the
    company's reputation at all times.
- **Practical demonstration:** Walk a site and identify what may and may not be photographed for job
  evidence, and how to secure the work zone.
- **Supervisor sign-off:** Applies the photo/confidentiality rules correctly and secures the work area.
- **Sample assessment questions:**
  - You need a before/after photo but a child's drawing with names is on the wall. What do you do?
  - Name two things you must never photograph on site.
- **Pass/fail:** Applies confidentiality and security rules with no breach. **Pass = 90%**
  (reputation-critical).
- **Evidence required:** Knowledge check; conduct sign-off; signed confidentiality acknowledgement.
- **Related:** `standards/site-cleanliness-security.md`.

## Module 11 — Job Completion, Evidence and Handover

- **Learning objective:** Finish a job properly — final inspection, pack-down, photo evidence, completion
  notes, defect reporting and client/site handover.
- **Why it matters:** If we cannot show what we did, we cannot defend the work or get paid cleanly. A
  clean handover is what turns a finished job into a renewed contract.
- **Key lessons:**
  - Final inspection against the job scope.
  - Pack-down: equipment, leads, hoses, signage, and leaving the area clean.
  - Photo evidence (within the confidentiality rules) and clear completion notes.
  - Defect reporting and the escalation process for anything outside the standard.
  - Client/site handover and sign-off.
- **Practical demonstration:** Complete a full close-out — inspection, pack-down, evidence, notes and
  handover — on a real or mock job.
- **Supervisor sign-off:** Closes out a job to standard with complete, rule-compliant evidence and a clear
  handover.
- **Sample assessment questions:**
  - What do you record when you find pre-existing damage during final inspection?
  - Who do you escalate to when a job cannot be completed to scope?
- **Pass/fail:** Complete close-out with compliant evidence and correct escalation. **Pass = 85%.**
- **Evidence required:** Completion notes; photo set; defect/escalation report where applicable;
  handover sign-off.
- **Related:** `checklists/site-arrival.md`, `02-risk-disruption-matrix.md`.

## Module 12 — Final Practical Assessment

- **Learning objective:** Demonstrate, on a full job, that you can work safely, correctly, professionally
  and independently to the company standard.
- **Why it matters:** This is the gate to working solo. It proves the standard holds when no one is
  guiding each step.
- **Key lessons (assessment, not new content):**
  - A complete job from pre-start to handover: vehicle/equipment readiness, site arrival and setup,
    surface identification, correct method and chemical, safe machine operation, hazard control,
    professional conduct, confidentiality/security, and close-out with evidence.
- **Practical demonstration:** Run a full standard job end to end, assessed by a supervisor against the
  checklists and standards.
- **Supervisor sign-off:** Confirms the new starter is competent to run standard jobs independently, with
  named escalation paths for anything outside the standard.
- **Sample assessment questions (scenario):**
  - Mid-job you find the surface is not what the scope said. Walk through what you change and who you tell.
  - A flat battery stops your scrubber halfway. What is your recovery and escalation?
- **Pass/fail:** Passes every safety-critical and reputation-critical checkpoint (no automatic-fail acts)
  and meets the standard on method and conduct. **Overall pass = 85%, with 100% on safety- and
  reputation-critical items.**
- **Evidence required:** Completed final assessment record; all checklists for the assessed job; photo
  evidence; supervisor independent-operator sign-off (see `04-pathways-30-60-90.md`).
