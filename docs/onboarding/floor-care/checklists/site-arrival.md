# Floor-Care Site Arrival, Sign-In, Briefing & Setup Checklist

Pillar 3. Run this every job, in order, from the moment you arrive until you confirm "ready to start".

> Australian English. Dates DD/MM/YYYY. Currency AUD.
> Work the steps in order. This checklist supports safe work and helps manage contaminants and soil load. It makes no medical, health, insurer or compliance-authority claims. Dilution rates, cure/re-entry times and slip-resistance details come from the SDS/TDS and relevant standards, not from this sheet.

## 1. Approach & parking

- [ ] Approached site safely; aware of pedestrians, children and traffic
- [ ] Parked per site rules [COMPANY TO CONFIRM parking rules]
- [ ] Vehicle not blocking exits, fire lanes, paths or access points
- [ ] Gear can be moved in without crossing busy or child areas where avoidable

## 2. Site sign-in

- [ ] Signed in using the site sign-in app/book [COMPANY TO CONFIRM]
- [ ] Site induction completed if required [COMPANY TO CONFIRM]
- [ ] ID/visitor pass worn and visible
- [ ] Noted sign-out requirement and emergency assembly point

## 3. Make contact with site contact

- [ ] Found and introduced yourself to the site contact
- [ ] Confirmed who to go to for access, questions and problems
- [ ] Confirmed contact's number/radio for the duration of the job

## 4. Take the job briefing

- [ ] Scope confirmed: which areas, which surfaces, what method
- [ ] Access confirmed: keys, doors, alarms, lifts, after-hours access
- [ ] Occupancy confirmed: who is on site, when areas are clear, peak times
- [ ] Water access located and confirmed usable
- [ ] Power access located; circuits/outlets confirmed [COMPANY TO CONFIRM]
- [ ] Any site-specific rules noted (no-go areas, quiet times, child areas)

## 5. Surface inspection

- [ ] Surface(s) identified before any product is selected
- [ ] Existing damage, marks or wear noted and photographed
- [ ] Method confirmed suitable for the surface (per Module 4/5 and SDS/TDS)
- [ ] Spot/test area considered where needed

## 6. On-the-spot risk assessment

- [ ] Hazards identified (slips, leads, machinery, chemicals, height, traffic)
- [ ] Occupancy assessed: are people in or moving through the area?
- [ ] **Children present?** If yes, plan to keep them fully clear of wet/work areas
- [ ] Wet-floor plan made: where will floors be wet, for how long, how kept clear
- [ ] Controls decided before starting; escalate if the job can't be made safe

## 7. Pre-start checks

- [ ] SDS checked for every product to be used
- [ ] Correct PPE on and in good condition
- [ ] Machines and leads checked; faults tagged out
- [ ] Signage, barricades and spill kit on hand
- [ ] Water and power confirmed working at the work point

## 8. Work-zone setup

- [ ] Wet-floor signage placed (supplementary administrative control only)
- [ ] Barricading / exclusion zones set up to separate people from wet and work areas
- [ ] Exits, paths and accessible routes kept clear at all times
- [ ] Leads and hoses routed, covered or taped to control trip hazards
- [ ] Ventilation arranged in occupied and child rooms where needed

> **Primary control = separating people from the wet/work area.** Signage supplements that separation; it does not replace it. If you can't keep people out, you are not ready to start.

## 9. Chemical placement

- [ ] Chemicals placed in a controlled spot, off paths, lids secure
- [ ] Chemicals **never** placed within reach of children or in child areas
- [ ] Decanted/diluted product correctly labelled
- [ ] Spill kit positioned and ready

## Ready-to-start gate

Do **not** start service execution until every box below is ticked.

- [ ] Signed in and site contact knows I'm here and what I'm doing
- [ ] Surface identified and method confirmed
- [ ] Risk assessment done; children and occupants planned for
- [ ] Pre-start checks passed; SDS checked; PPE on
- [ ] Work zone set up; people separated from wet/work areas; exits clear
- [ ] Chemicals placed safely and away from children
