# Chemical & PPE Checklist

**Purpose:** Make sure the right PPE is worn and chemicals are carried, labelled, stored and used safely on every floor-care job — driven by the product SDS, not by memory.

> **If it is not checked, it can stop the job.** A missing glove, an unlabelled bottle, an out-of-date SDS or two incompatible chemicals stored together can shut a job down or cause harm. Check it before you start.

> **PPE is the last resort.** PPE sits at the bottom of the hierarchy of controls. Wherever you can, eliminate or reduce the hazard first (ventilate, dilute correctly per the SDS/TDS, choose a less hazardous product, use the machine instead of hand application). PPE is what protects you when the hazard can't be removed — not the first answer.

> **VIC note:** In Victoria, work under the *Occupational Health and Safety Act 2004* and its regulations. Confirm local procedures. **[COMPANY TO CONFIRM]**

# Part A — PPE

## A1. PPE Matrix  *(a guide — always select the actual PPE from the product SDS)*

> The matrix below is a starting point. **The SDS for the specific product you are using is the source of truth** for glove material, eye protection and respiratory protection. If the SDS calls for more, wear more.

| Task | Gloves | Eye | Respiratory | Footwear | Other |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| General floor cleaning | Per SDS (chemical-resistant) | Safety glasses if splash risk | Usually none — read SDS | Closed, slip-resistant | Hi-vis on site |
| Acid tile / grout cleaning | Per SDS (acid-resistant, e.g. nitrile/neoprene) | Goggles / face shield | Per SDS (acid vapour) — ventilate | Chemical-resistant, slip-resistant | Apron if splashing |
| Caustic / alkaline stripping | Per SDS (caustic-resistant, gauntlet length) | Goggles / face shield | Per SDS — ventilate | Chemical-resistant boots | Apron / coveralls |
| Sealing (solvent or water-based) | Per SDS (solvent-resistant if solvent base) | Safety glasses | Per SDS (solvent vapour) — ventilate | Closed, slip-resistant | Knee pads as needed |
| Pressure washing | Per SDS + impact | Goggles / face shield (spray-back) | Per SDS if chemical added | Waterproof, slip-resistant | Waterproofs, hearing protection if noisy |

*All "Per SDS" entries: read the current SDS for the exact product before starting. Cure times, re-entry times, dilution rates and any slip-rating considerations come from the SDS/TDS and the relevant standards — not assumed here.*

## A2. PPE Condition Check  *(before each task)*

- [ ] Correct PPE for the task selected from the product's SDS
- [ ] Gloves correct material, right size, no tears or thin spots
- [ ] Eye protection clean, not scratched, fits and seals
- [ ] Respiratory protection (if required) correct type, filters in date, seals to face
- [ ] Footwear closed and slip-resistant; chemical-resistant where the task needs it
- [ ] Other items (apron, coveralls, hearing protection) present and serviceable
- [ ] Damaged or expired PPE set aside and replaced — not used

# Part B — Chemicals

## B1. SDS & Register

- [ ] A current SDS is available for every chemical carried / used today
- [ ] Each SDS is no more than 5 years old (request an updated one from the supplier if older) **[COMPANY TO CONFIRM supplier accounts]**
- [ ] Hazardous chemicals register is accessible to workers (vehicle and/or site)
- [ ] SDS read for any unfamiliar product before use — hazards, PPE, ventilation, first aid, spill, storage

## B2. Labelling

- [ ] Every container is labelled — including all decanted containers
- [ ] Labels are on the **body of the container, not the lid**
- [ ] Decant labels identify the product and its hazards
- [ ] A decanted container used immediately and then emptied and cleaned out does not need a permanent label — otherwise it must be labelled
- [ ] No unlabelled bottles anywhere on the vehicle or site

## B3. Segregation & Storage

- [ ] Incompatible chemicals segregated — acids kept apart from alkalis and oxidisers, per each SDS's storage advice
- [ ] Chemicals stored upright, sealed, and restrained so they can't tip or shift in transit
- [ ] Storage away from heat, ignition sources and direct sun as the SDS advises
- [ ] On site: chemicals kept in a controlled area, away from the public, children and food
- [ ] Containers returned to the vehicle's segregated storage at the end of the job

## B4. Ventilation in Occupied / Child-Occupied Rooms

- [ ] Read the SDS exposure information for the product before working in an occupied area
- [ ] Ventilate to control airborne contaminants — open windows / doors, use fans or extraction as needed
- [ ] In rooms used by children, the public or sensitive occupants, confirm ventilation and re-entry approach against the SDS before starting
- [ ] Respiratory PPE worn where the SDS / conditions require it (ventilation first, PPE as backup)

## B5. Spill Response

- [ ] Spill kit on hand and matched to the chemicals carried
- [ ] If a spill occurs: stop the source, keep people clear, and put on the PPE the SDS specifies
- [ ] Contain the spill (absorbent / bunding) before it spreads or reaches drains
- [ ] Clean up and dispose per the SDS — never wash unknown / hazardous product to stormwater
- [ ] Restock the spill kit and report the spill **[COMPANY TO CONFIRM fault/incident reporting]**

## B6. The "Never" List

- [ ] **Never** use or carry an unlabelled bottle
- [ ] **Never** mix chemicals (e.g. acid with bleach/alkali) — they can react and release hazardous gas
- [ ] **Never** leave a decanted container for another worker without a clear label
- [ ] **Never** reuse a food / drink container for chemicals
- [ ] **Never** guess dilution or re-entry — get it from the SDS/TDS
