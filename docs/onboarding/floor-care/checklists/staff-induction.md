# Floor-Care Staff Induction Checklist

Day 1-2 new-starter induction. Complete every item before the worker is sent to a live site.

> Australian English. Dates DD/MM/YYYY. Currency AUD.
> This induction supports hygiene and safe work practices. It makes no medical, health, insurer or compliance-authority claims. Cure times, re-entry times, dilution rates and slip-resistance details come from the product SDS/TDS and the relevant standards, not from this sheet.

## 2. Company standards walkthrough — the 7 standards

Walk the new starter through each standard. Tick when explained and understood.

- [ ] **Turn up ready** — right gear, right paperwork, on time, fit for work
- [ ] **Know the surface** — identify the floor before you touch it; wrong method damages floors
- [ ] **Safety built-in** — PPE, SDS check and risk assessment happen every job, not just when it's convenient
- [ ] **Protect people on site** — children, staff and public come first; separate them from wet and work areas
- [ ] **Leave it clean** — tidy as you go, remove your gear, hand the area back better than you found it
- [ ] **Protect private info / assume CCTV** — say nothing about clients; behave as if you are always recorded
- [ ] **Prove the work** — before/after photos, sign-off and handover so the job stands up later

## 3. PPE issue & fit

- [ ] Gloves issued (correct type for the chemicals used; check the SDS)
- [ ] Eye protection issued
- [ ] Footwear suitable for wet/slippery floors confirmed
- [ ] Hearing protection issued where machinery requires it
- [ ] Respiratory protection issued where the SDS calls for it [COMPANY TO CONFIRM stock]
- [ ] Each item checked for fit and condition with the worker
- [ ] Worker shown how to inspect, clean, store and replace PPE
- [ ] Worker told who to see when PPE is worn out or doesn't fit

## 4. Uniform issue [COMPANY TO CONFIRM]

- [ ] Uniform issued and sizes recorded [COMPANY TO CONFIRM]
- [ ] Hi-vis issued where required
- [ ] ID/lanyard issued [COMPANY TO CONFIRM]
- [ ] Presentation standard explained (clean, tidy, no offensive items)
- [ ] Worker told how to launder, replace and return uniform

## 5. SDS & chemicals register orientation

- [ ] Shown where the SDS register lives (vehicle and/or app) [COMPANY TO CONFIRM]
- [ ] Shown how to find the SDS for a product **before** using it
- [ ] Walked through reading an SDS: hazards, PPE, first aid, spill, storage
- [ ] Told that dilution rates, cure/re-entry times come from the SDS/TDS — never guessed
- [ ] Shown correct labelling of decanted/diluted product
- [ ] Shown safe chemical storage and segregation in the vehicle
- [ ] Shown spill response and where the spill kit is

## 6. Confidentiality & child-safe conduct acknowledgement

- [ ] Confidentiality expectations explained (no photos of people, no site gossip, no social media)
- [ ] Child-safe conduct explained: never alone with a child, no inappropriate contact, report concerns
- [ ] Told how and to whom to report a child-safety or conduct concern [COMPANY TO CONFIRM]
- [ ] "Assume CCTV" expectation understood
- [ ] Worker has read and signed the confidentiality & child-safe conduct acknowledgement

## 8. Who to call / escalation [COMPANY TO CONFIRM]

- [ ] After-hours / urgent contact given [COMPANY TO CONFIRM]
- [ ] Emergency services + site emergency procedure explained
- [ ] Incident/near-miss reporting process explained [COMPANY TO CONFIRM]
- [ ] Worker knows the order: make safe → secure people → call for help → report

## 9. Tour of vehicle & equipment

- [ ] Vehicle walk-around: condition, load, securing of gear
- [ ] Pre-start vehicle checks explained (tyres, lights, fluids, leaks) [COMPANY TO CONFIRM]
