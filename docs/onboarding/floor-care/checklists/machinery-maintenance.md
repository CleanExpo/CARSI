# Machinery Maintenance & Fault Reporting Checklist

**Purpose:** Keep floor-care machines, batteries, hoses and leads working and safe through daily, weekly and monthly maintenance — and make sure faults get reported and faulty gear gets tagged out of service.

> **If it is not checked, it can stop the job.** A machine that won't start, a flat battery, a split hose, a damaged lead or a missing tool can fail a job tomorrow. Maintenance done today is the next job protected.

> **Out-of-service rule:** Any item found unsafe or faulty is **tagged out and removed from use** until it is repaired or replaced. Do not "make do" with damaged electrical gear or a damaged machine.

> **VIC note:** In Victoria, work under the *Occupational Health and Safety Act 2004* and its regulations. **[COMPANY TO CONFIRM]**

# Part D — FAULT REPORTING & OUT-OF-SERVICE

## D1. What to Log

- [ ] Item / machine ID and description **[COMPANY TO CONFIRM asset IDs]**
- [ ] Date and time the fault was found (DD/MM/YYYY)
- [ ] What is wrong (symptoms — won't start, leaking, damaged lead, low suction, etc.)
- [ ] How it was found (during pre-start, in use, during maintenance)
- [ ] Whether it was tagged out of service
- [ ] Your name

## D2. Who to Tell

- [ ] Report the fault to the supervisor / maintenance contact promptly **[COMPANY TO CONFIRM contact & method]**
- [ ] Log it in the fault-report system **[COMPANY TO CONFIRM system]**
- [ ] For anything affecting today's jobs, tell the supervisor before departing or before the next job

## D3. Tagging Out of Service

- [ ] **Tag out** any item that is unsafe, faulty, or fails its checks
- [ ] Attach an "Out of Service — Do Not Use" tag with date and reason
- [ ] Remove the item from the working load so it can't be picked up by mistake
- [ ] Any electrical item with a damaged lead, plug or failed RCD is tagged out immediately
- [ ] Item stays out of service until repaired / replaced and, where electrical, re-tested per AS/NZS 3760

> **Out-of-service rule (repeat):** If it is unsafe, it does not go back on the truck. No exceptions, no "make do."
