# Program Overview — Professional Floor Care Onboarding & Operational Readiness

> Part of **CARSI Maintenance Company Onboarding**. Operating company: **CARSI Maintenance Company**.
> Delivered to client organisations as a subscription — **AUD $1,295/month + GST, unlimited students**.

> This onboarding program teaches staff that professional floor care is not just cleaning. It is a
> safety, hygiene, presentation, asset-protection, and operational-readiness service. Every
> technician must understand the surfaces, chemicals, equipment, risks, site expectations, and
> professional standards required to deliver consistent results in sensitive facilities.

---

## 1. Executive summary

CARSI Maintenance Company delivers professional floor surface cleaning, polishing, sealing, maintenance
and hygiene services
across sensitive environments — schools, childcare and education facilities, public buildings,
commercial sites and high-traffic facilities. In these places the floor is one of the most used,
most visible and highest-risk surfaces on site. The way we clean and maintain it affects hygiene,
slip safety, the look of the facility, the life of the floor, and the client's confidence in us.

This program brings every new technician, supervisor and facilities staff member to a single
standard. It teaches:

1. **What** professional floor surface care is.
2. **Why** it matters — hygiene, safety, presentation, asset protection.
3. **How** our company performs the service, step by step.
4. **What** professionalism we expect on every site.
5. **How** safety, hygiene, presentation, maintenance and operational readiness decide whether each
   job succeeds.

The program is delivered as 12 modules with practical demonstrations, supervisor sign-off, and a
final practical assessment, mapped to a 30/60/90-day pathway from new starter to independent
operator. It is built to be issued as an induction pack and a training manual today, and to become an
LMS course, with SOPs and toolbox talks, next.

**Outcome:** a new starter who can turn up ready, identify the surface, choose the right method and
chemical, work safely around children and the public, deliver a consistent result, leave the site
clean and secure, and hand over with evidence — without a supervisor standing over them.

## 2. The problem this program solves

Floor-care work fails in predictable ways, and most of them are not about cleaning skill:

- A technician arrives without the right pad, brush, chemical or PPE, and the job stalls.
- The vehicle or TruckMount is not ready, so the team cannot get to site or cannot run the equipment.
- The wrong chemical or method is used on a surface and damages it — a cost we wear.
- A wet or just-sealed floor is left without signage or barricading, and someone slips.
- A technician behaves unprofessionally on a school or childcare site, and we lose the contract.
- Private information is seen, discussed or photographed, and we breach the client's trust.
- The job is finished but there are no completion notes or photos, so we cannot prove what we did.

Every one of these is preventable with a clear standard, a checklist and basic operational readiness.
This program installs that standard so the result does not depend on who turns up that day.

## 3. The standards we hold

These are the non-negotiables. Every module and checklist comes back to them.

1. **Turn up ready.** Vehicle, machines, tools, chemicals, PPE and paperwork are checked before you
   leave. *If it is not checked, it can stop the job.*
2. **Know the surface before you touch it.** Identify the floor and whether it is sealed or porous,
   then choose the method and chemical to match. When unsure, stop and ask.
3. **Safety is built into the job, not added later.** Read the SDS, wear the right PPE, control the
   area, sign and barricade wet floors, manage leads and hoses, and ventilate occupied rooms.
4. **Protect the people on site.** We work around children, staff, teachers, visitors and the public.
   Behave so a parent, principal or facility manager watching would be reassured.
5. **Leave it better, and leave it clean.** Our own work area stays clean, safe and organised. No
   mess, no spills, no uncontrolled leads or hoses, nothing left behind.
6. **Protect private information and the company's reputation.** Assume CCTV is running and act like
   every action is visible to the client. Do not discuss or photograph private site information.
7. **Prove the work.** Final inspection, photo evidence, completion notes, defect reporting and a
   clear handover on every job.

We use careful language about what floor care does. It **supports** hygiene, **reduces** soil load,
**helps manage** dust, pollen, soils and contaminants, and **contributes to** safer, better-presented
indoor environments. We do not make medical claims, and we do not claim to sterilise or guarantee
health outcomes.

## 4. Program outline

The program is built in three layers: **the standards** (this document and the standards files), **the
training** (12 modules), and **the tools** (checklists, the risk matrix, the assessment framework and
the 30/60/90 pathway).

### The 12 modules

1. Introduction to Professional Floor Care
2. Health, Hygiene and Child-Sensitive Environments
3. WHS, PPE, SDS and Chemical Safety
4. Floor Surface Identification
5. Cleaning, Polishing, Sealing and Maintenance Methods
6. Machinery and Tool Operation
7. Vehicle, Machine and Equipment Maintenance
8. Site Setup and Hazard Control
9. Professional Conduct and Client Expectations
10. Confidentiality, Security and CCTV Awareness
11. Job Completion, Evidence and Handover
12. Final Practical Assessment

Each module (set out in full in `01-module-structure.md`) has a learning objective, a "why it
matters", key lessons, a practical demonstration, supervisor sign-off, sample assessment questions,
pass/fail requirements and the evidence required.

### The tools

- **Induction and sign-off:** `checklists/staff-induction.md`, `checklists/supervisor-sign-off.md`
- **On the job:** `checklists/vehicle-equipment-pre-start.md`, `checklists/site-arrival.md`,
  `checklists/chemical-ppe.md`, `checklists/machinery-maintenance.md`
- **Standards:** `standards/floor-surface-identification.md`, `standards/professionalism-conduct.md`,
  `standards/site-cleanliness-security.md`
- **Operational control:** `02-risk-disruption-matrix.md`, `03-assessment-framework.md`
- **Progression:** `04-pathways-30-60-90.md`
- **Rollout:** `05-implementation-recommendations.md`

### How a new starter moves through it

1. **Induction (Day 1–2):** paperwork, PPE issue, the standards above, and the conduct and security
   rules — before going to a live site.
2. **Supervised work (to Day 30):** modules and demonstrations, always paired with a competent
   operator. Learns the checklists by doing them.
3. **Competency (to Day 60):** runs jobs with lighter supervision; supervisor signs off each module.
4. **Independent operator (to Day 90):** completes the final practical assessment and is cleared to
   run standard jobs solo, with escalation paths for anything outside the standard.
