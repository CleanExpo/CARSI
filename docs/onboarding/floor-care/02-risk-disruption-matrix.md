# Risk and Disruption Prevention Matrix

> Most failed or delayed floor-care jobs are **not** caused by poor cleaning. They are caused by
> something that stopped the job before or around the work — the vehicle, a machine, a missing tool,
> a missing chemical, bad planning, or bad communication. **If it is not checked, it can stop the
> job.** This matrix names the common disruptions and how we prevent and escalate each one.

## How to use this

- **Technicians:** the *Prevention* column is mostly things on your daily pre-start and site-arrival
  checklists. Do them and most of this never happens.
- **Supervisors / operations:** use the *Escalation* column to respond fast when a job is at risk, and
  review repeat disruptions at toolbox talks.
- **Escalation contacts and timeframes** are **[COMPANY TO CONFIRM]** — fill in the named roles and
  phone numbers before issue.

Severity key: 🔴 stops the job · 🟠 delays/limits the job · 🟡 risks quality or reputation.

## The matrix

### 1. Vehicle failure 🔴
- **Cause:** No daily vehicle check; low fuel/charge; tyre, lighting or mechanical fault; load not
  secured; service overdue.
- **Impact:** Team cannot reach site, or arrives late; the whole day's run is disrupted.
- **Prevention:** Daily vehicle pre-start (`checklists/vehicle-equipment-pre-start.md`); keep fuel/charge
  above the company minimum [COMPANY TO CONFIRM]; report defects the day they appear; keep service up to
  date.
- **Escalation:** Notify supervisor immediately; arrange backup vehicle or re-allocate the run; advise
  the affected site of revised arrival time [COMPANY TO CONFIRM contact].

### 2. Machine failure (rotary, scrubber, burnisher, extractor, wet vac, pressure system) 🔴
- **Cause:** No pre-start check; motor/pump fault; blocked or worn parts; flat battery; damaged cord.
- **Impact:** Cannot perform the method on site; partial or aborted job.
- **Prevention:** Machine pre-start checks; daily/weekly/monthly maintenance
  (`checklists/machinery-maintenance.md`); tag out faulty machines; carry a backup where the run depends
  on one machine.
- **Escalation:** Switch to backup/alternate method if safe and in scope; if not, notify supervisor and
  re-book; raise a fault report and tag the machine out of service.

### 3. Missing tools 🟠
- **Cause:** Tool inventory not reconciled against the job list; left on the last site; not restocked.
- **Impact:** Job cannot be completed to scope; return trip needed.
- **Prevention:** Tool inventory vs today's job list on the pre-start; pack-down inventory before leaving
  every site; restock consumables at end of day.
- **Escalation:** Borrow/collect if quick and safe; otherwise notify supervisor, complete what is in
  scope, and re-book the remainder with notes.

### 4. Missing or low chemicals 🟠
- **Cause:** Stock not checked; used more than expected; not reordered.
- **Impact:** Cannot complete the method; substitution risk (wrong chemical for the surface).
- **Prevention:** Chemical stock check on the pre-start (enough for the day, sealed, labelled); reorder
  before run-out [COMPANY TO CONFIRM supplier]; never substitute an unsuitable chemical to "get it
  done."
- **Escalation:** Notify supervisor; do not improvise on sensitive surfaces; re-book the affected work.

### 5. Incorrect pads or brushes 🟡
- **Cause:** Consumables not matched to the surfaces/methods booked for the day.
- **Impact:** Poor result, or surface damage from an over-aggressive pad/brush.
- **Prevention:** Match pads/brushes to the job list on the pre-start; know pad/brush selection (Module 6
  and `standards/floor-surface-identification.md`); carry common spares.
- **Escalation:** Do not proceed with the wrong consumable on a delicate surface; collect the correct one
  or re-book; flag the gap to the supervisor.

### 6. Incorrect or missing PPE 🔴
- **Cause:** PPE not issued, not packed, damaged, or not matched to the chemical/task per the SDS.
- **Impact:** Unsafe to perform the task; job must stop; injury risk.
- **Prevention:** PPE on the pre-start and the chemical/PPE checklist (`checklists/chemical-ppe.md`);
  select PPE from the SDS; check condition; carry spares.
- **Escalation:** **Do not perform a task without the PPE its SDS requires.** Notify supervisor; obtain
  correct PPE or re-book.

### 7. Incomplete job notes / evidence 🟡
- **Cause:** Final inspection, photos, completion notes or defect report skipped or rushed.
- **Impact:** Cannot prove the work; disputes; pre-existing damage wrongly attributed to us; payment
  delays.
- **Prevention:** Close-out discipline (Module 11); photo evidence within the confidentiality rules;
  complete notes and defect reports every job.
- **Escalation:** If evidence cannot be captured (e.g. confidentiality), record why in the notes and tell
  the supervisor; never invent or omit silently.

### 8. Poor site access planning 🟠
- **Cause:** Access points, keys, alarm codes, lift/after-hours access or occupancy not confirmed before
  arrival.
- **Impact:** Cannot get in or get equipment to the work area; lost time; aborted job.
- **Prevention:** Confirm access, keys/codes and occupancy in the job briefing [COMPANY TO CONFIRM
  access process]; check TruckMount/equipment can physically reach the area.
- **Escalation:** Contact the site contact on arrival; if no access, notify supervisor and re-book; log
  the access requirement for next time.

### 9. Poor parking planning 🟡
- **Cause:** No plan for where to park or run hoses/leads; blocked access; disrupting the site.
- **Impact:** Long hose/lead runs (trip hazards), disruption to site operations, or inability to set up.
- **Prevention:** Confirm parking and unload point in the briefing (`checklists/site-arrival.md`,
  `standards/professionalism-conduct.md`); plan hose/lead routes that don't cross pedestrian paths.
- **Escalation:** Ask the site contact for an alternative; if unsafe, adjust the method or re-book.

### 10. No water access 🟠
- **Cause:** Water point not confirmed; not working; too far from the work area.
- **Impact:** Cannot run extraction/pressure methods; partial job.
- **Prevention:** Confirm water access in the briefing; carry capacity where the method needs it
  [COMPANY TO CONFIRM]; identify the nearest point on arrival.
- **Escalation:** Use carried water if available and in scope; otherwise notify supervisor and re-book.

### 11. No power access 🟠
- **Cause:** Power point not confirmed/working; RCD trips; insufficient circuits for the equipment.
- **Impact:** Cannot run electric machines; job stalls.
- **Prevention:** Confirm power access in the briefing; test the portable RCD; check leads on the
  pre-start; know battery-machine alternatives.
- **Escalation:** Switch to battery equipment if in scope; if a circuit/RCD keeps tripping, **stop** and
  report a possible electrical fault — do not bypass safety devices.

### 12. Unexpected floor condition 🟠
- **Cause:** Surface, soiling, damage or sealing state different from the scope.
- **Impact:** Booked method/chemical is wrong or unsafe; risk of surface damage.
- **Prevention:** Surface inspection and on-the-spot risk assessment before starting (Module 4 & 8);
  test an inconspicuous area; know surface/chemistry limits.
- **Escalation:** **Stop before damage.** Photograph and document; contact the supervisor to confirm a
  revised method or re-scope; do not guess.

### 13. Poor communication 🟡
- **Cause:** Delays, risks or issues not raised early; site contact not kept informed.
- **Impact:** Surprised client; eroded trust; avoidable complaints.
- **Prevention:** Communicate delays/risks/issues early and plainly
  (`standards/professionalism-conduct.md`); confirm the briefing and the handover.
- **Escalation:** Tell the site contact and the supervisor as soon as an issue is known — early and
  honest beats late and defensive.

### 14. Staff lateness 🟠
- **Cause:** No buffer; poor route planning; pre-start done on site instead of before departure.
- **Impact:** Late arrival; compressed work window; flow-on delays to later jobs.
- **Prevention:** Pre-start completed before departure; plan the run with realistic travel/buffer; notify
  early if running late [COMPANY TO CONFIRM start times].
- **Escalation:** Call ahead to the site and supervisor with a revised time; re-sequence the run if
  needed.

### 15. No pre-start checklist completed 🔴
- **Cause:** Pre-start skipped or rushed — the root cause behind many of the disruptions above.
- **Impact:** Compounds every other risk; the day starts with unknowns.
- **Prevention:** Pre-start is mandatory and signed off before departure
  (`checklists/vehicle-equipment-pre-start.md`). **If it is not checked, it can stop the job.**
- **Escalation:** Supervisor spot-checks pre-start records; repeated misses are a competency and conduct
  issue, reviewed against the assessment framework.

### 16. Lack of spare parts / backup equipment 🟠
- **Cause:** No spares carried for the items that commonly stop a job; single point of failure on one
  machine.
- **Impact:** A small fault (pad, fuse, hose fitting, key chemical) stops the whole job.
- **Prevention:** Carry the standard spares kit [COMPANY TO CONFIRM] — spare pads/brushes, fuses, hose
  fittings, key chemicals, a backup machine where the run depends on one.
- **Escalation:** Use spares; if none, notify supervisor and re-book; flag the spares gap so the standard
  kit is updated.

## Reading the pattern

Almost every row above is prevented by **two habits**: the **daily pre-start** before departure and the
**site-arrival sequence** on arrival. The disruptions that are not (unexpected floor condition, poor
communication) are prevented by **inspecting before you start** and **speaking up early**. Train these
four habits and the disruption rate drops sharply. Repeat disruptions should be logged and reviewed —
see `05-implementation-recommendations.md` for the toolbox-talk loop.
