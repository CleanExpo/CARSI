# Module 13 — Strip & Seal (Resilient & Hard Floors)

*New module extending Module 8 (Hard-Floor Cleaning & Inspection) into the strip-and-seal discipline.
Australian-produced · IICRC S220 inspection-aligned · verifiable. Safety-first: strippers are strong
alkalis and sealers are wet-slip hazards.*

Module 8 is inspection-first and teaches you to **clean** hard floors safely (the vinegar drop test, the
calcareous-vs-siliceous rule, the non-destructive inspection scope). **Strip and seal** is the next
service up: removing spent floor finish and laying fresh protective coats on **resilient** floors. It is
one of the highest-value add-ons a carpet cleaner can bolt on — the customer already trusts you, and the
equipment overlap (rotary/CRB, extractor, wet-vac) is large. It also carries the **highest chemical and
slip risk** of anything in the workshop, so it is taught as a disciplined sequence, not a knack.

---

## Where Strip & Seal Belongs — and Where It Does Not

| Floor | Strip & seal? | Why |
|---|---|---|
| **Vinyl sheet / VCT (vinyl composition tile)** | **Yes** — the core case | Designed for stripped-and-sealed acrylic finish |
| **Linoleum (true lino)** | Yes, with care | Alkali-sensitive — use lino-safe stripper, never high-pH flood |
| **LVT (luxury vinyl tile/plank)** | Usually **no top-coat** | Most LVT has a factory wear layer; clean + polish-protect, do not strip |
| **Sealed timber / laminate** | **No** | Water + alkali swells joints; refinishing is a joiner's trade |
| **Marble / limestone / travertine** | **No** — never acid | Calcareous; stone sealing is a separate stone-care discipline |
| **Ceramic / porcelain + grout** | Grout seal only | Tile itself is not stripped; penetrating grout sealer is a related add-on |

> **The discipline rule.** Strip & seal is a **resilient-floor** service — chiefly **VCT and vinyl
> sheet**. If you are not certain the floor is resilient and finish-coated, you inspect first (Module 8)
> and you do **not** strip. A stripped timber or etched marble floor is a five-figure mistake.

## The Chemistry, in One Breath

- **Stripper** = a **strong alkali** (often with a solvent booster) that softens and dissolves the old
  acrylic/metal-cross-linked finish so it can be lifted off. High pH — PPE-critical.
- **Neutraliser / rinse** = a **mild acid or neutral rinse** that brings the substrate back to neutral so
  the new finish bonds. Skip this and the fresh sealer **will not adhere** and will powder off.
- **Sealer / finish** = a water-based **acrylic (or metal-cross-linked) polymer** laid in thin coats that
  self-level and cure to a protective, buffable film.

## The Sequence — Nine Steps, In Order

Every step assumes AU site conditions: **230 V / 10 A GPO** power, **RCD-protected** leads, wet-floor
signage up from the first drop of stripper.

1. **Inspect & confirm resilient.** Identify the floor (Module 8). Check for lifting tiles, asbestos-risk
   old VCT (pre-1990s vinyl-asbestos tile is a **stop-and-refer** — do not disturb), and moisture.
2. **Protect the site.** Wet-floor signs, cordon the area, mask skirting/edges, lift or protect adjacent
   carpet. Ventilate.
3. **Dust-mop / vacuum** all loose grit — grit under a pad scratches the substrate.
4. **Apply stripper** to a manageable section at the label dilution; let it **dwell** (do not let it dry —
   re-wet if it flashes off). Work in sections you can finish before the stripper dries.
5. **Agitate** with a rotary/CRB and a stripping pad (or a stiff deck brush in corners/edges). The finish
   should turn to a milky slurry.
6. **Extract / pick up the slurry** with a wet-vac or extractor immediately — before it re-deposits and
   dries back onto the floor.
7. **Neutralise & rinse** with a neutral or mild-acid rinse; pick up; **repeat the clean-water rinse**
   until the floor squeaks clean and pH-tests neutral. This is the step amateurs skip and pros never do.
8. **Dry fully.** The substrate must be **bone dry** before sealer — trapped moisture clouds the finish
   and wrecks adhesion. Airmovers help.
9. **Apply sealer in thin, even coats**, edges first then figure-eight fill, letting each coat cure per
   the label (typically **2–5 coats**). Optionally **burnish** high-speed once cured for gloss.

> **CARSI EXPERT NUGGET · The Neutral Rinse Is the Whole Job**
> Nine times out of ten, a strip-and-seal that fails — finish that powders, peels, or never gains gloss —
> failed at **step 7, not step 9**. Stripper residue is alkaline; fresh acrylic finish needs a neutral
> substrate to cross-link and bond. Leave 20% of the stripper behind and the customer sees a beautiful
> floor for a fortnight, then a chalky, streaky one. The rinse water going down clear and a pH strip
> reading neutral are the two checks that separate a job that lasts a year from a callback in three weeks.

> **DID YOU KNOW · Old VCT can contain asbestos.** Vinyl-asbestos tile was widely laid in Australian
> schools, halls and shops before the late 1980s. Aggressive stripping and machine-sanding of unknown old
> tile can release fibres. If a tiled resilient floor looks pre-1990s and its provenance is unknown, that
> is a **stop, do-not-disturb, and refer for assessment** — not a job you push through. Safe Work
> Australia's asbestos rules govern here, not the finish manufacturer.

## Safety — Strip & Seal Specifics

- **PPE:** nitrile gloves (chemical-grade), **safety glasses/goggles**, non-slip closed footwear, P2
  respirator if the product or ventilation warrants. Strippers blister skin and sting eyes.
- **Slip:** a stripped or freshly-sealed floor is extremely slippery — **signage and cordon** until fully
  cured and walk-safe.
- **Electrical:** RCD-protected leads only; keep leads and machine bodies out of the slurry; 230 V + water
  is an electrocution risk managed by the safety switch.
- **SDS** on site for the stripper, neutraliser and sealer in use; decanted containers labelled.
- **Ventilation** for solvent-boosted strippers.

## Pricing the Service *(ties to Module 10)*

Strip & seal prices **per square metre** and is labour- and coat-count-heavy. The two costing traps:

- **Coat count** — a 4-coat finish is roughly double the finish-labour of 2 coats; quote the coat count.
- **The rinse/dry time** — the neutral rinse and full dry are unbillable-looking but non-negotiable;
  price them in or you subsidise them out of margin (see Module 10, "the drying trade-off" applied to
  hard floors).

## Three-Tier Take-Aways

**NEWBY** — Strip & seal is a **resilient-floor** (VCT/vinyl) service. Never strip timber, laminate or
stone. Signage and PPE from the first drop. Old unknown tile = stop and refer (asbestos).

**INTERMEDIATE** — Run the nine-step sequence; never skip the neutral rinse; confirm bone-dry before
sealer; lay thin even coats and quote the coat count.

**PRO** — Diagnose LVT-vs-VCT and finish-vs-no-finish on sight; read adhesion failures back to the rinse
step; price coat-count and cure-time honestly; recognise asbestos-risk substrates before machinery touches
them.
