# Module 14 — Add-On Services: The Profit Multiplier

*New module extending Module 10 (Business of Cleaning). Australian-produced · IICRC-aligned · verifiable.
Every service here is inspection-led, disclosed honestly, and priced — never a pressure upsell.*

Module 10 teaches the six cost lines and three pricing models. This module answers the question that
decides whether a run of jobs is profitable or merely busy: **what else can you honestly sell on the visit
you are already standing in?** The economics are simple and powerful — you have already paid the most
expensive cost line (travel, setup, the customer's trust). A second and third service on the **same
visit** carries almost no new overhead, so its margin is far higher than the first.

> **The add-on principle — inspection-led, not pressure-led.** A CARSI add-on is offered because the
> **inspection surfaced a genuine need or opportunity**, disclosed plainly with the choice left to the
> customer. "Your traffic lanes will re-soil fast without protector — here's what it does and costs;
> your call" is a service. Invented urgency and phantom problems are not — and are prohibited under the
> Australian Consumer Law (misleading conduct). Sell the truth; the truth on a trusted visit sells well.

---

## Why Add-Ons Multiply Margin *(the one-visit maths)*

The first service on a visit absorbs the fixed costs: drive time, setup, pack-down, the minimum call-out.
A second service added while you are **already on site** skips almost all of that. The result: your
**average job value** rises much faster than your **cost per visit** — which is the single most reliable
lever a small operator has on take-home profit (Module 10, cost lines 4–6 barely move; line 1 moves a
little; the fee moves a lot). Track **average services-per-visit** as a KPI alongside callback rate.

## The CARSI Add-On Menu

Grouped by how naturally they attach to a carpet-cleaning visit. Each needs the matching skill/credential
and is quoted, not assumed.

### Tier 1 — Attaches to almost every carpet job
| Service | What it is | Natural trigger on inspection |
|---|---|---|
| **Carpet protector** | Fluorochemical/fibre protector re-applied after cleaning | Worn traffic lanes; kids/pets; light-colour carpet |
| **Deodorising** | Odour treatment (pairs with OCT skill) | Pet, smoke, damp, general "musty" |
| **Spot & stain treatment** | Targeted stains beyond the standard clean | Any pre-existing stain flagged at pre-inspection |
| **Sanitising / anti-allergen** | Antimicrobial / dust-mite treatment | Asthma/allergy households, aged care, childcare |

### Tier 2 — Same site, different surface (equipment overlap)
| Service | What it is | Natural trigger |
|---|---|---|
| **Upholstery cleaning** | Lounge/sofa/dining chairs (Modules 6–7) | Matching soiling on soft furniture |
| **Mattress cleaning & sanitising** | Dust-mite + stain treatment | Bedrooms, allergy households |
| **Tile & grout cleaning** | High-pressure hard-floor clean (Module 8) | Kitchens, bathrooms, entries |
| **Grout sealing** | Penetrating sealer after grout clean | Porous/stained grout lines |
| **Strip & seal** | Resilient-floor finish renewal (Module 13) | VCT/vinyl in shops, halls, offices |
| **Rug cleaning** | Area/wool/oriental rugs (points toward RCT) | Loose rugs on the same job |

### Tier 3 — Specialist / seasonal / commercial
| Service | What it is | Natural trigger |
|---|---|---|
| **Pet treatment package** | Urine decontamination + deodorise + protector | Pet households (see Module 12) |
| **Vehicle & marine interior** | Automotive/boat carpet & upholstery | Startup packages already include auto tools |
| **End-of-lease / bond clean carpets** | Documented clean to a standard | Rental turnover — huge AU market |
| **Commercial maintenance plan** | Scheduled interim + restorative cadence | Any commercial site (points toward CCMT) |
| **Post-event / hospitality** | Rapid turnaround venue carpets | Function centres, clubs |

> **DID YOU KNOW · Australia's end-of-lease market is a standing add-on.** With roughly a third of
> Australian households renting, **bond/end-of-lease carpet cleaning** is a continuous, documented,
> repeatable job type — often with a receipt the tenant *needs* for their bond. It attaches to the same
> skills you already have and rewards a clean, dated, photographed report (your Module 4 drying discipline
> and Appendix D checklist become the deliverable).

## Which Add-On Needs Which CARSI/IICRC Credential

Add-ons are also your **CE pathway in disguise** (Appendix G). Each credential unlocks a service you can
charge for on a visit you are already making:

| Add-on | Skill/credential that backs it |
|---|---|
| Deodorising | **OCT** — Odour Control Technician |
| Upholstery / mattress | **UFT** — Upholstery & Fabric Cleaning Technician |
| Rug cleaning | **RCT** — Rug Cleaning Technician |
| Commercial maintenance plans | **CCMT** — Commercial Carpet Maintenance Technician |
| Colour/bleach-spot repair | **CRT** — Colour Repair Technician |
| Strip & seal, tile & grout | Hard-floor competence (Module 8 + 13) |

A CCT-only tech can clean. A **CCT + UFT + OCT** tech can clean, upholster **and** deodorise — and charge
for all three on one visit (Appendix G). That is the credential-stacking argument made concrete.

## How to Present an Add-On *(the honest script)*

1. **Find it at inspection**, not mid-clean. Note it on the Appendix D pre-inspection sheet.
2. **Show the customer** the actual condition (the traffic lane, the stained grout, the pet spot).
3. **State plainly** what the service does, what it costs, and what happens if they decline. No urgency
   theatre.
4. **Leave the choice** with them. Record accept/decline on the job sheet.

> *"While I had the machine out I noticed the grout in your entry is holding soil the mop can't reach — I
> can high-pressure clean and seal it today for $X, or leave it; entirely your call."* Genuine, specific,
> priced, optional. That is the whole technique.

## Three-Tier Take-Aways

**NEWBY** — Learn the menu; offer only what you can competently deliver; find add-ons at inspection and
disclose them honestly. Never invent a problem.

**INTERMEDIATE** — Track **services-per-visit** and average job value; attach Tier-1 protector/deodorise
to most jobs; quote each service with its own line.

**PRO** — Build credential-stacked packages (clean + upholstery + deodorise), design commercial
maintenance cadences, and own repeatable AU job types (bond cleans, commercial plans) that fill the diary.
