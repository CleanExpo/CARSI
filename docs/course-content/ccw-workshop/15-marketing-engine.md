# Module 15 — The Marketing Engine: YouTube → Social → Local Discovery

*New module extending Module 10 (Business of Cleaning) into how a cleaner actually gets found and chosen
in 2026. Australian-produced · verifiable. Every statistic below is sourced (see "Sources"); anything not

Technical excellence with an empty diary is a hobby. Module 10 protects your margin **per job**; this
module fills the **diary** those jobs come from. It is built on one insight the trade under-uses: for a
local service business, **video connected across your channels is now the front door** — and almost none
of your competitors are doing it well.

## The 2026 Reality — What the Data Says

Three findings that decide where a cleaner's marketing hour is best spent. Each is sourced and — this is
the CARSI standard — **carries its provenance and its limits with it**, so you can weigh it honestly:

- **The "Local Pack" is where local customers land.** In a **2026 WebFX study of 100 US home-services
  keywords** (500 searches), the Google **Local Pack** (the top-3 map block) appeared in **98% of
  searches** — far more consistently than paid ads — and it is the one placement you **earn** (through
  your Google Business Profile, reviews and relevance) rather than **buy**. *Read it as directional for an
  Australian carpet-cleaning business: US market, home-services broadly (HVAC/plumbing/roofing), not
  carpet-specific — but the "own your Local Pack" lesson travels.* *(WebFX, 2026, primary-verified.)*
- **Video is the new front door — and almost no one uses it.** On Google Business Profiles, **videos are
  reported to be viewed about 2× more often than photos, while only ~4% of profiles use video.** That gap
  is the single biggest under-exploited local-marketing opening available to a cleaner right now.
  *(SOCi / Publer — publisher-reported; directional, not independently audited.)*
- **A complete profile with photos correlates with more engagement.** Google states customers are
  **70% more likely to visit** and **50% more likely to consider purchasing** from a business with a
  **complete Business Profile**, and that profiles **with photos get 42% more direction requests**.
  *(Google-attributed. Honesty note: the 70%/50% figures are ubiquitous but Google no longer publishes
  them at a current dated URL — treat them as Google-attributed directional claims, not independently
  audited metrics. The defensible takeaway stands on its own: complete, photo-rich, video-carrying
  profiles engage more.)*

> **The founder's insight, formalised.** *"YouTube needs to be connected to their social media pages."*
> Exactly — because the video that earns trust on YouTube should also be **surfacing on the map listing,
> the Facebook page, the Instagram feed and the website**, from one filming session. A cleaner who films
> one honest before/after and lets it appear across every channel is doing, deliberately, what 96% of
> profiles are not doing at all.

## The Connected System — One Filming Session, Every Channel

Think of it as a hub and spokes. **YouTube is the library; Google Business Profile is the shopfront; the
social feeds are the reminders; the website closes the sale.** Connect them once and each new video feeds
all of them.

```
                         ┌─────────────────────┐
                         │  ONE 30-sec video    │  (before/after, filmed on the job)
                         └──────────┬──────────┘
                                    │
        ┌──────────────┬────────────┼─────────────┬──────────────┐
        ▼              ▼            ▼             ▼              ▼
   YouTube        Google Business  Facebook     Instagram      Website
   (the library)  Profile (shop)   Page         (Reels)        (service page)
        │              │            │             │              │
        └──── links back to the matching service page + phone ───┘
```

### How to actually connect them (the wiring)
1. **Create/claim the four accounts** under one business identity: a **YouTube channel**, a **Google
   Business Profile**, a **Facebook Page**, an **Instagram** (Business) account.
2. **Link Instagram to the Facebook Page** (Meta Business account) so a post/Reel publishes to both. *(A
   cleaner's most common gap — the two Meta channels are not linked, doubling the work.)*
3. **Add video to the Google Business Profile**, not just photos — this is the 4% move above.
4. **Put your YouTube channel link** on the website, the Facebook Page, the Instagram bio and the GBP.
5. **Every video description and post links back to the matching service page** on your site — a tile &
   grout clip links to the tile & grout page with the phone number, **not** the homepage.

## The Content Playbook — What to Film

Authenticity beats production value for home services — homeowners trust job-site footage over studio ads.
*(E-Business Nest, 2026; SOCi.)* You do **not** need a studio; you need a **repeatable weekly rhythm** —
**one useful video a week is enough** for most local brands. *(ClickyOwl, 2026.)*

**The five clips that work (film them as you already work):**

| Clip type | What to show | Job it does |
|---|---|---|
| **Before / after** | The traffic lane / stain, then the result | Proof — the trade's strongest asset |
| **"Did you know" tip** | One 20-sec fact (e.g. blot-not-rub, urine-on-rainy-days) | Authority + repeat views |
| **Service in action** | A safe part of the process — the wand pass, the strip-and-seal slurry | Expertise, demystifies the job |
| **The verification clip** | Van, uniform, tools, a street sign/landmark, one live action | GBP trust + local proof |
| **Result / walkthrough** | The finished room, honest and unstaged | Conversion support |

**The format that holds attention:** ~**3 seconds of location → 15–20 seconds of the work → a few seconds
of the finished result**, under **30 seconds**, phone held steady, no long intro. *(ClickyOwl, 2026.)*

> **DID YOU KNOW · You are sitting on the content already.** Every job you do is a filmable before/after.
> The barrier is never material — it is **rhythm**. The operators who win local attention in 2026 are
> simply the ones who look the **most real, most consistently**: one honest clip a week, filmed on the
> job, surfaced across all five channels. That is a marketing engine most competitors will never build
> because it feels too simple to matter.

## The Image & Asset Plan *(the "images, etc." brief)*

For each service you want to sell, capture and publish, from real jobs (customer permission on file, no
private details in frame):

- **A hero before/after still** (for the website service page and GBP photo set).
- **The 30-second video** (YouTube + GBP video + Facebook + Instagram Reel).
- **3–5 GBP photos per category** — Google recommends exterior, interior, team, equipment, results.
  *(Google Business Profile Help.)* Businesses with **customer photos get 42% more direction requests.**
  *(SOCi, 2026.)*
- **A branded thumbnail** consistent with CARSI/your-business colours (design, not luck — this is where a
  marketing-minded owner adds real value).

*Australian note:* film and photograph in AU homes/workplaces, AU vehicles, AU signage; keep it locally
recognisable — the whole point of local discovery is that you look local.

## Measurement — Prove It Worked *(so this isn't guesswork)*

- **Add UTM tags** to the website link on your GBP and social bios so profile traffic shows up in
  analytics. *(ClickyOwl, 2026.)*
- **Track the actions that pay:** profile calls, website clicks, direction requests, form fills — and
  **lead quality** after each upload.
- **Judge over 60–90 days, not one week** — video builds click-confidence and familiarity over time, not
  overnight. *(ClickyOwl, 2026.)*
- **Reviews compound:** there is roughly a **3% lift in GBP conversions for every 10 new reviews**, so a
  post-job review request belongs in the same rhythm as the weekly video. *(SOCi.)*

## Compliance — Market Honestly *(the CARSI line, non-negotiable)*

Everything you publish is subject to the **Australian Consumer Law**: claims must be truthful and
substantiated, testimonials genuine, before/after images from the **same real job** (never a stock or
swapped "after"). Any incentive or connection behind a review must be **disclosed**. This is the same
prove-it-or-cut-it standard the rest of the workshop runs on — your marketing carries it too. Sell the
truth; on a trusted local visit, the truth is your best salesperson.

## Three-Tier Take-Aways

**NEWBY** — Claim the four accounts, link Instagram to Facebook, add **video** to your Google Business
Profile, and film one honest before/after this week.

**INTERMEDIATE** — Run the weekly one-video rhythm across all five channels from one filming session; link
