2. Extends the cadence to **six intervals** — Daily · Weekly · Monthly · Quarterly · Half-Year ·
   End-of-Year — plus the **hours-based triggers** that override the calendar when you are busy.

## The Six-Interval Care System

Calendar intervals keep a low-volume operator honest. **Hours-based triggers** override the calendar
when you are flat out — a machine run 8 hours a day hits its service hours long before the month ends.
Whichever comes first, wins.

| Interval | Portable trigger | Truck-mount trigger | Purpose |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Daily** | Every job / end of day | Every job / end of day | Flush chemistry, protect vacuums, catch failures early |
| **Weekly** | ~5 working days / 15–20 h | ~5 working days / 20–30 h | Filters, hoses, benchmark suction & pressure |
| **Monthly** | ~20 days / 60–80 h | ~50–100 engine h | Deep-flush chemical system, seals, valves |
| **Quarterly** | ~200–250 h | ~300 engine h (Kohler EFI oil) | Descale, pump service, drive components |
| **Half-Year** | ~400–500 h | ~600 engine h / mid-season | Vacuum-motor brushes, blower, heat exchanger |
| **End-of-Year** | Annual / 1000 h | Annual / full service | Technician inspection, log reconciliation, winterise |

## Archetype A — In-Tank-Heat Portable
*(SPOTX, TRACTX, Creed 220, Balboa 220 / 500LW)*

**Daily — before first job & at day's end (5 min)**
- Fill the tank and run the pump; check the **spray-fan pattern** at the wand — a split or narrow fan is
  a blocked jet.
- Clean the **vacuum inlet filter / shut-off float screen** inside the recovery tank. Confirm the float
  ball moves freely (a stuck float lets water reach the vacuum motors and blow out the exhaust).
- End of day: **rinse the solution tank, chemical line and pump with fresh water** — never leave
  pre-spray or acidic rinse sitting in the pump overnight.
- Rinse the recovery tank; wipe the drain-valve gasket clean so it seats without premature wear.
- **Store with the recovery-tank lid open** so it dries and does not sour.

**Weekly (15 min)**
- Remove and clean the **solution filter** (a weekly minimum — more often on hard water).
- Inspect **hoses and cuffs** for cracks, weep leaks and loose fittings.
- Benchmark **water-lift** at the wand against your new-machine baseline; a drop means a leak or a
  tired vacuum motor.
- Wipe the poly housing with mild soap and water.

**Monthly (30 min)**
- **Flush the chemical system** with a manufacturer maintainer to clear detergent gum.
- Clean the **air inlet**; check the exhaust port is clear.
- Check the **dump-valve / drain seal** for wear.

**Quarterly (~250 h) — the descale (60 min)**
- **Flush a white-vinegar solution through the solution system** to strip alkaline / hard-water scale:
  roughly **1 L white vinegar to 8 L water** (≈ 1 qt : 2 gal), run it through, then rinse with clean
  water. Scale is the number-one silent killer of in-tank heaters and jets. *(OEM guidance across
  Clarke, Mytee & Esteam portable manuals.)*
- Inspect the **in-tank immersion heater** element for scale crust; descale as above if coated.
- Service the **pump inlet filter**; check pump output pressure against baseline.

**Half-Year (~500 h) — the motor pass (90 min)**
- **Pull and measure the vacuum-motor carbon brushes.** New ≈ 25 mm; **condemn at ≈ 8 mm.**
  Replace *before* failure — a worn brush that arcs the commutator writes off a motor that costs
  10–15× the price of a $15–40 brush pair. *(Manual, Module 9 nugget — applied here on a schedule.)*
- Inspect vacuum gaskets and the recovery-tank lid seal.

**End-of-Year (annual / 1000 h)**
- Qualified-technician inspection; replace pump seal kit if hours met; reconcile the machine log.

## Archetype B — Dual Heat System (DHS) Portable
*(Creed 700 DHS, Balboa 500LW DHS, Balboa 700)*

Everything in Archetype A **plus** care for the **inline heat exchanger**, which recovers waste-vacuum
heat to boost solution temperature.

**Daily** — after the fresh-water flush, **run clean water through the DHS exchanger** so no chemistry
cools and hardens inside the coil.

**Quarterly** — descale the **exchanger coil** as well as the in-tank heater (vinegar flush). A scaled
exchanger silently loses the temperature advantage you paid for and the operator never notices because
the machine still *runs*.

**Half-Year** — inspect exchanger inlet/outlet fittings for scale weep and corrosion.

> **DID YOU KNOW · A DHS coil fails "quietly"**
> An in-tank heater that dies gives you cold water — an obvious fault. A **scaled DHS exchanger**
> keeps working, just worse: the solution arrives a few degrees cooler each month as scale insulates the
> coil. Because CHAT lets heat trade against dwell and chemistry, the operator unconsciously compensates
> — longer dwell, stronger pre-spray — and never suspects the machine. The quarterly vinegar flush is
> what keeps the temperature you *think* you have the temperature you *actually* have.

## Archetype C — Auto Fill / Auto Pump-Out Portable
*(Drago 1600 Auto, Balboa 700 Auto)*

Everything in A/B **plus** care for the **auto-dump pump-out** and **auto-fill float**.

**Daily** — clean the **debris screen on the auto-dump pump-out**; lint that passes the waste filter
collects at the pump base.

**Fortnightly** — **run the auto-dump with a full tank of clean water** to flush accumulated lint from
the pump base. *(Sandia / auto-pump-out OEM guidance.)*

**Monthly** — test the **auto-fill float and shut-off** for correct cut-in / cut-out; a stuck float
either starves the pump or overflows.

**Quarterly** — inspect the pump-out check valve and discharge hose for build-up.

## Archetype D — Slide-In Truck-Mount
*(Sapphire 370EFI / Peak 500 / Apex 570 / Everest 870HP · HydraMaster TITAN / Boxxer / TMTG4000)*

A truck-mount is an **engine + blower + heat exchanger + high-pressure pump** on a shared frame.
Engine hours drive the schedule — a machine on 6-hour cleaning days accrues service hours fast.

**Daily — end of shift**
- **Flush the solution system and high-pressure pump with clean water**; leave no chemistry in the pump.
- Empty and rinse the **waste tank**; clean the waste-filter and pump-out screen.
- Check **engine oil level** on the sight glass/dipstick; check coolant on liquid-cooled units (Kubota /
  Everest).
- Visual: belts, hoses, fuel line, no leaks under the console.

**Weekly (20–30 h)**
- Benchmark **vacuum (in Hg)** and **solution pressure (psi)** at the wand against baseline.
- Inspect **blower belt** tension and glazing; listen for new vibration or pitch from the blower.
- Clean water-box / float filters; inspect chemical-metering filter.

**Monthly (~50–100 engine h)**
- **Flush the heat exchanger**; visually inspect for corrosion.
- Test the **vacuum relief valve**.
- Grease per schedule; check all electrical connections for corrosion.

**Quarterly — the engine-oil interval that trips people up (~300 h)**
- On **Kohler EFI** engines (Sapphire 370EFI etc.) with the extended-oil-life programme, change **oil and
  filter every 300 hours** using the approved synthetic oil — *no 8-hour break-in change*. Most other
  truck-mount engines need a **first change at 8 h then every 50–100 h** — so **know which engine you
  own** before you skip an oil change. *(Sapphire / Kohler published spec, ccwonline.com.au.)*
- Rebuild-kit check on the high-pressure solution pump if hours met.
- Descale the heat exchanger (annual minimum; quarterly on hard water).

**Half-Year (~600 h / mid-season)**
- Blower oil change and bearing check per manufacturer hours.
- Inspect Cerakote exhaust shielding and cooling-belt / heat-exchanger coil.
- Vacuum-hose and solution-hose full inspection; replace worn cuffs.

**End-of-Year**
- Full manufacturer-schedule service; reconcile hours log; **winterise** (anti-freeze through lines) in
  any frost-risk region before the first cold snap.

> **CARSI EXPERT NUGGET · The 300-Hour Trap**
> The single most expensive truck-mount maintenance error is applying the *wrong engine's* oil interval.
> An operator who upgrades from an older machine to a Sapphire 370EFI often keeps their old habit of an
> 8-hour break-in change and 50-hour intervals — harmless, just wasteful. The far costlier mistake runs
> the other way: an operator told "change every 300 hours" applies it to a **non-EFI** engine that
> actually needs a change every 50–100 hours, and cooks the motor. The rule is not a number you memorise
> — it is **read your engine plate, then follow that engine's manual.** Log the engine model on the front
> page of your maintenance book so a relief driver cannot get it wrong.

## Hours-Based Trigger Card (print for the van)

| Component | Check / service at | Condemn / act |
|---|---|---|
| Vacuum-motor carbon brushes | Measure every 400–500 h | Replace at ≈ 8 mm (new ≈ 25 mm) |
| In-tank heater / DHS coil | Descale every ~250 h or on hard water | Visible scale crust |
| Solution / HP pump | Pressure check weekly; rebuild per OEM hours | Output psi below baseline |
| Kohler EFI engine oil | Every **300 h** (approved synthetic) | — |
| Non-EFI truck-mount oil | First **8 h**, then **50–100 h** | — |
| Blower belt | Weekly inspect | Glazed / cracked / slack |
| Hoses & cuffs | Weekly inspect | Crack, weep, loose cuff |

## Three-Tier Take-Aways

**NEWBY** — Find your machine's archetype. Do the Daily flush every day without fail. Never run a
portable without the vacuum inlet filter in place.

**INTERMEDIATE** — Run the six-interval calendar *and* the hours card, whichever trips first. Log
baselines and compare weekly. Descale on schedule, not on symptom.

**PRO** — Cost downtime against preventive spend; plan brush, pump and belt replacements 6–12 months
out; know your exact engine's oil interval and train relief drivers to the log, not to habit.
