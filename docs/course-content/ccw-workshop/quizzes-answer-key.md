## Module 9+ — Machine-Specific Maintenance

1. **10 A** (Balboa 700 Auto single cord) and **15 A** (larger TRACTX units).
2. **Every 300 hours** (Kohler EFI extended-oil-life, approved synthetic, no 8-hour break-in). The trap:
   applying "300 h" to a **non-EFI** engine that actually needs a first change at 8 h then every
   50–100 h — which cooks the motor. Rule: read the engine plate, follow that engine's manual.
3. Condemn at **≈ 8 mm** (new ≈ 25 mm). Replace before failure because a worn brush arcs the commutator,
   pits the windings and **writes off a motor costing 10–15× the $15–40 brush pair**.
4. **White vinegar**, ~1 L to 8 L water, flushed through the solution system quarterly to remove
   **alkaline / hard-water scale** from heaters, jets and lines.
5. A **scaled DHS heat-exchanger coil** — it still runs but delivers cooler solution each month, so the
   operator unconsciously adds dwell/chemistry (CHAT) to compensate. The **quarterly vinegar flush** of
   the coil prevents it.
6. Cleaning the **vacuum inlet filter / shut-off float screen** and confirming the **float ball moves
   freely** — a stuck float lets water reach and destroy the vacuum motors.

## Module 12 — Advanced Stain Removal

1. A **chromophore** is the part of a molecule that absorbs light and makes a stain visible. **Oxidisers**
   switch it off by **adding oxygen** to break it; **reducing agents** by **removing oxygen**.
2. **Red dye** (cordial / sports drinks) on a heat-tolerant synthetic fibre — reducing-agent removers are
   **heat-activated by design**. It is dangerous on **protein** (blood/egg/dairy) because heat **cooks and
   permanently sets** protein.
3. **Urine salts are hygroscopic** — they draw moisture from humid air, so on damp days the deposit
   re-wets and the odour returns; the salts were never removed from the underlay/sub-floor.
4. A dedicated **oxalic-acid / rust remover** (strong acid). Keep it **off calcareous stone** (marble,
   limestone, travertine) — acid etches it permanently.
5. **No** — it is **colour loss (a bleach spot)**, not a stain; no cleaner restores it. The fix is
   **colour repair / re-dyeing** (IICRC CRT), or refer.
6. **Aqueous spotter → acidic spotter → (rinse) → oxidiser → rinse-extract.** (Tannin is the textbook
   oxidiser stain; rinse between rungs.)

## Module 13 — Strip & Seal

1. Appropriate for **resilient floors — VCT / vinyl sheet** (with care on true lino). **Never strip**
   sealed **timber/laminate** or **calcareous stone** (marble/limestone/travertine) — pick any two.
2. **Step 7 — the neutral rinse.** Leftover **alkaline stripper residue** stops the fresh acrylic finish
   cross-linking/bonding, so it powders and peels within weeks.
3. Stripper is a **strong alkali (high pH)**; the neutral rinse returns the substrate to **neutral** so
   the sealer **adheres and cures** properly.
4. **Stop and refer for assessment — do not disturb.** Pre-1990s vinyl tile may be **vinyl-asbestos**;
   aggressive stripping/sanding can release fibres (Safe Work Australia asbestos rules).
5. Trapped moisture **clouds the finish and destroys adhesion** — the sealer must bond to a dry substrate.
6. Any two of: **wet-floor signage + cordon** (extreme slip on stripped/sealed floor), **chemical goggles
   for high-pH stripper**, **P2 respirator/ventilation for solvent-boosted stripper** (a carpet-only job
   needs none of these).

## Module 14 — Add-On Services

1. The first service absorbs the **fixed costs** (travel, setup, minimum call-out); a second service on
   the **same visit** adds almost no new overhead, so its **margin is far higher**.
2. A legitimate add-on is **inspection-led, disclosed honestly, priced, and optional**; a prohibited
   upsell uses **invented urgency/phantom problems**. The **Australian Consumer Law** (misleading/deceptive
   conduct) makes it matter.
3. Deodorising → **OCT**; rug cleaning → **RCT**; upholstery → **UFT**.
4. **End-of-lease / bond carpet cleaning** (rental market); the valuable deliverable is a **clean, dated,
   photographed report/receipt** the tenant needs for their bond.
5. Identified **at inspection** (before cleaning), recorded on the **Appendix D pre-inspection sheet /
   job sheet** (accept or decline).
6. **Average services-per-visit** (equivalently, average job value).

## Module 15 — The Marketing Engine

1. **~98%** of home-services searches (WebFX 2026). It is **earned** (via Google Business Profile, reviews,
   relevance) rather than **bought** like paid ads.
2. GBP **videos are viewed ~2× more than photos**, but only **~4%** of profiles use video. The gap matters
   because it is a **high-impact move almost no competitor is making** — easy differentiation.
3. **YouTube channel, Google Business Profile, Facebook Page, Instagram (Business).** **Instagram and the
   Facebook Page** must be linked so one post/Reel publishes to both.
4. **~3 s location → 15–20 s of the work → a few seconds of result; under 30 seconds**, steady phone, no
   long intro.
5. **60–90 days** (not one week). Track any two of: **profile calls, website clicks, direction requests,
   form fills, lead quality** (with UTM tags on the website link).
6. Any two: before/after images must be from the **same real job** (no stock/swapped "after"); reviews
   must be **genuine**; any **incentive or connection** behind a review must be **disclosed**; all claims
   **truthful and substantiated**.
