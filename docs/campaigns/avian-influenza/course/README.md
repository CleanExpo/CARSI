# CARSI Course Package — Australian H5 Bird Flu Readiness

## Course title

Australian H5 Bird Flu Awareness for Restoration, IAQ and Facility Professionals

## Course status

Draft v1 for subject-matter review and LMS conversion.

## Audience

This course is for restoration, cleaning, indoor air quality, facility, strata, insurance-support and professional remediation teams who may receive questions about H5 bird flu, sick or dead birds, field documentation, building contamination concerns or client communication.

## Course promise

This course helps professionals communicate calmly, report correctly, protect workers, stay within scope, document field work and avoid unsupported claims.

## What this course is not

This course is not veterinary training, wildlife handling training, public-health diagnosis, government biosecurity response training or product endorsement training.

## Current official-source message to preserve

- Australia has reported H5 bird flu detections in migratory seabirds.
- The public should avoid contact with sick or dead birds or animals.
- The public should record what they see and report it to the Emergency Animal Disease Hotline on 1800 675 888.
- Australian Government and Australian CDC wording must be checked before publication and corrected if official guidance changes.

## Course structure

1. Official message and reporting pathway.
2. Professional role, scope and boundaries.
3. Worker safety, PPE awareness and clean/dirty zones.
4. Cleaning-before-disinfection principles and product-claim limits.
5. RestoreAssist.app documentation pathway.
6. Client, media and social communication.
7. Knowledge check and completion statement.

## Required completion standard

Learners must score at least 80% on the knowledge check and agree to the professional-boundary statement:

> I understand that my role is to report, protect, document and communicate within scope. I do not diagnose bird flu, handle wildlife without authorisation, certify sites as disease-free or make product claims that are not supported by label, SDS, regulator position and evidence.
