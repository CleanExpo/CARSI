# Facilitator Guide — Australian H5 Bird Flu Awareness

## Purpose

This guide helps CARSI facilitators, reviewers and Synthex campaign operators deliver the course consistently.

## Tone standard

Use professional, restrained language. The course should sound like a public-interest briefing, not a sales presentation.

Preferred language:

- readiness;
- reporting;
- worker protection;
- documentation;
- official-source guidance;
- professional boundaries;
- evidence-led product discussion.

Avoid language:

- crisis;
- panic;
- guaranteed;
- government-approved product;
- disease-free certification;
- kills H5 in all cases;
- required control measure;
- fastest or best without proof.

## Opening facilitator script

Welcome to the CARSI Australian H5 Bird Flu Awareness course for restoration, IAQ, cleaning and facility professionals.

This course exists to help industry professionals communicate clearly, stay within scope and protect workers. The course does not replace government, veterinary, wildlife or public-health authorities. The first public message is always: do not touch sick or dead birds, record what you see and report to the Emergency Animal Disease Hotline on 1800 675 888.

## Discussion prompts

### Prompt 1 — Public report

A client says there is a dead bird outside a commercial entry. What do you say first?

Expected discussion:

- do not touch;
- keep people and pets away;
- record the location;
- call 1800 675 888;
- document the call and advice received;
- do not diagnose the site.

### Prompt 2 — Facility manager pressure

A facility manager wants a contractor to clear the bird and disinfect the area immediately.

Expected discussion:

- confirm whether authority reporting has occurred;
- do not handle wildlife without authorisation;
- separate observation, reporting, cleaning and disinfection scopes;
- record limitations and authority pathway;
- do not make unsupported product claims.

### Prompt 3 — Product question

A client asks whether dry fogging is required.

Expected discussion:

- CARSI does not claim dry fogging is required by Australian Government H5 control measures;
- dry fogging may only be discussed as label-led, SDS-led, evidence-backed and authority-aware;
- cleaning and source control come before disinfection discussion;
- document product, label, SDS, contact time and limitations where product use is within scope.

## Facilitator caution

If learners ask for veterinary, wildlife-handling, medical diagnosis, public-health direction or government-response instructions, redirect to the relevant authority and keep CARSI training within professional restoration and documentation scope.

## Assessment review

A learner should not pass if they repeatedly:

- says contractors should handle sick or dead birds without authorisation;
- makes disease-free certification claims;
- says product use is required without authority evidence;
- removes the hotline from public messaging;
- uses panic or unsupported outbreak wording;
- treats RestoreAssist.app as a regulatory certification tool.

## Completion statement

At completion, learners should be able to say:

> I can repeat the public reporting message, explain my professional boundaries, document field work, avoid unsupported claims and communicate calmly with clients.
