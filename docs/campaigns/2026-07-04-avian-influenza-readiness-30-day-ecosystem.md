Date: 2026-07-04  
Primary hub: `/avian-influenza-readiness`  
Campaign tone: calm, official-source, evidence-led, no panic messaging

## Editorial position

This campaign must reflect the Australian Government position, not speculation and not fear-based marketing.

Core wording to repeat across all platforms:

- As of 9am AEST, 5 July 2026, DAFF reported six confirmed H5 bird flu cases in wild birds: four in Western Australia, one in South Australia and one in New South Wales.
- DAFF states there is no evidence of mass mortality and no evidence of infection in poultry or the wider agriculture industry.
- The Australian Centre for Disease Control states the current risk to people in Australia is considered low.
- The public action is to avoid contact, record what is seen and report sick or dead birds or animals to the 24-hour Emergency Animal Disease Hotline on 1800 675 888.

## Claim guardrails

Do say:

- Report sick or dead birds or animals to 1800 675 888.
- Do not touch sick or dead birds or animals.
- Professional restoration and IAQ members can assist with risk assessment, documentation, containment planning, cleaning records, disinfection records and client communication under authority direction.
- Cleaning before disinfection is a core control principle.
- Dry fogging, Halo, Halosil and NeoSan-related training must be label-led, SDS-led and legally compliant.

Do not say:

- Australia has a mass outbreak.
- Poultry, food supply or agricultural production is infected unless DAFF states that.
- A product kills H5N1 unless the claim is on-label, legally valid in Australia and supported by evidence.
- Dry fogging is required by Australian Government control measures.
- CARSI, RestoreAssist, DisasterRecovery.com.au or NRPG replaces government, veterinary, wildlife or public-health authorities.

## Halo / Halosil / dry fog method language

The correct phrasing is:

> Hydrogen peroxide dry-fog systems may be considered as controlled, post-cleaning, label-dependent hard-surface disinfection tools in vacant and risk-assessed spaces. They are not a substitute for official biosecurity response, source removal, pre-cleaning, PPE, waste control, ventilation, re-entry safety or product-label compliance.

SOP phrase:

> Clean first. Disinfect second. Document third.

Required SOP elements:

1. Authority and biosecurity status check.
2. Site WHS risk assessment and exposure-control plan.
3. Exclusion zone and clean/dirty transition.
4. Source removal and gross contamination management under authority direction.
5. Detergent cleaning before disinfection.
6. Product registration, label and SDS check.
7. Machine volume calculation, fogging plan and vacant-space control.
8. No occupancy during application unless product documentation allows otherwise.
9. Re-entry criteria and ventilation.
10. RestoreAssist report with product, batch, dilution, dwell/contact time, operator, PPE and exceptions.
