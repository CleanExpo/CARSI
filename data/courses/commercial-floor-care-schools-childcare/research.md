# Research Pack — Commercial Floor Care for Schools & Childcare Contracts

> Stage 1 (`/course-research`) artifact. Verified, source-cited ground truth for the later
> stages. en-AU throughout. Claims below carry a confidence rating; anything that could not
> clear the publish threshold is in **Unverified / excluded** and must not be taught as fact.

## Course identity

- **Proposed slug:** `commercial-floor-care-schools-childcare`
- **Working title:** Commercial Floor Care for Schools & Childcare Contracts
- **Domain:** Commercial cleaning (floor care) — carpet, tile & grout, vinyl strip & seal, pressure washing.
- **IICRC discipline:** Not a core restoration discipline. Closest certification is **CCT (Carpet Cleaning Technician)** for the carpet module; the rest is general commercial floor care + WHS/contract practice. **CEC relevance: low/none** — position as professional development & contract-readiness, **not** as an IICRC-accredited CEC course (no overclaiming).
- **Duplication check:** No existing CARSI course covers commercial floor care, schools, or childcare (the catalogue is restoration/air-quality/drying). Clear differentiator — no duplicate.

## Learner frame

- **Persona:** Australian commercial cleaning operator / technician, or a small cleaning-business owner, who wants to win and reliably service **school and childcare centre** floor-care contracts.
- **Prior knowledge:** Practical cleaning experience; variable WHS/compliance literacy; little formal grounding in slip-resistance standards, SDS duties, trade-waste rules, or childcare/school contract obligations.
- **On-the-job outcome:** Can scope, quote, schedule and safely deliver carpet, tile, vinyl strip-&-seal and pressure-washing work in occupied/child-occupied facilities, with the documentation a contract expects.
- **Single next action the course should drive:** Build a compliant, child-safe floor-care service offer (method + SDS register + slip-resistance verification + run-off control + scheduling) ready to put to a school/childcare client.

## Course thesis / differentiator

Most floor-care training teaches *technique*. The contract value in schools/childcare is **technique + compliance + child-safety + documentation**. This course pairs each of the four service areas with the AU regulatory frame (WHS/SDS, slip resistance, run-off, childcare/school standards) and grounds chemical/equipment choices in a **real Australian product catalogue with current SDS** (CCW / Actichem etc.).

## Verified claims (source + confidence)

### 1. WHS duties for hazardous chemicals (model WHS / SafeWork Australia)
- **1.1 (High)** Model WHS Act & Regulations (Safe Work Australia, 2011) only have legal effect once each state/territory or the Commonwealth adopts them. *SWA — Model CoP: Preparation of SDS.*
- **1.2 (High)** A PCBU that uses/handles/stores hazardous chemicals must **obtain and keep current SDS** and keep them accessible to workers at the workplace. *SWA — Using safety data sheets.*
- **1.3 (High)** SDS must be reviewed/revised **at least every 5 years**; "current" = not more than 5 years old, and each SDS states its last-reviewed date. *SWA — Preparing SDS.*
- **1.4 (High)** A **hazardous chemicals register** (with a current SDS for each chemical) must be prepared, maintained and accessible — model WHS Reg 346. *SWA — Hazardous chemicals register.*
- **1.5 (High)** Hazardous chemicals (including **decanted/transferred** containers) must be correctly labelled per the model WHS Regs. *SWA — WHS duties / labelling.*
- **1.6 (High)** A **manifest** is only required above Schedule 11 thresholds — generally **not** triggered by ordinary floor-care volumes. *SWA — Manifest requirements.*
- ⚠️ **VIC operates under its own OHS Act/Regulations**, not the model WHS framework — flag for VIC contracts. Direct learners to their state regulator (SafeWork NSW, WorkSafe Vic, WHSQ).

### 2. Slip resistance (AS 4586 / AS 4663)
- **2.1 (High)** **AS 4586:2013** classifies slip resistance of **new** pedestrian surfaces (lab/pre-installation). *Standards Australia.*
- **2.2 (High)** **AS 4663:2013** measures slip resistance of **existing** surfaces (on-site/in-service). *Standards Australia.*
- **2.3 (Medium)** Wet-pendulum test → **SRV** classified into **P0–P5** (P5 highest); oil-wet ramp test gives **R9–R13**. *SA HB 198:2014 guidance.*
- **2.4 (Medium)** **SA HB 198:2014** gives recommended minimum classifications by location type; NCC references it for new building work. *Standards Australia / Building CodeHub.*
- **2.5 (Medium)** Because AS 4663 applies to existing floors, it is the **correct basis to re-verify slip resistance after stripping/sealing**. *Standards Australia.*
- ⚠️ AS 4586/4663 **classify/measure only — they do NOT set cure or re-entry times.** Do not imply otherwise.

### 3. Working With Children Checks (contractors in schools/childcare)
- **3.1 (High)** WWC checks are legislated/administered **per state & territory** (8 schemes); required for child-related work, which includes contractors at schools/childcare. *Office of the Children's Guardian / NSW Parliament summary.*
- **3.2 (High)** Names vary: NSW **WWCC**, VIC **Working with Children Check**, QLD **Blue Card**, ACT **WWVP registration**. *QLD Gov.*
- **3.3 (High)** Generally **not auto-transferable** between jurisdictions (e.g., QLD doesn't recognise interstate checks). *QLD Gov.*
- ⚠️ Keep high-level. A WWC check may **not** be the only screening a site requires (additional child-safe induction). Do not present it as sufficient on its own.

### 4. Childcare hygiene (NQF/NQS) & school cleaning standards
- **4.1 (High)** The **NQF** operates under the Education and Care Services National Law & National Regulations; **ACECQA** oversees the NQF/NQS. *ACECQA.*
- **4.2 (High)** **NQS Quality Area 2** (Children's health & safety) — minimise risk, protect from harm/injury/**infection**; Standards 2.1 (Health) & 2.2 (Safety). *ACECQA.*
- **4.3 (High)** NQS elements map to enforceable provisions of the National Regulations. *ACECQA Guide to the NQF.*
- **4.4 (High)** NSW public schools are cleaned under a **whole-of-government cleaning contract** with published performance standards. *School Infrastructure NSW.*
- **4.5 (Medium)** Periodic hard-floor strip/seal and carpet extraction in schools is typically scheduled into **deep-clean / holiday** programs, not daily routines. *School Infrastructure NSW + industry restatement.*

### 5. Pressure-washing wastewater / run-off
- **5.1 (High)** Stormwater drains flow **untreated to waterways** — "the drain is just for rain." *EPA NSW / Sydney Water.*
- **5.2 (High)** Wash run-off (detergents/contaminants) **must not enter stormwater**; confine the area, collect wastewater, direct to **sewer**. *Sydney Water — contaminated surface water.*
- **5.3 (High)** Discharging trade wastewater to sewer needs **prior written approval (trade-waste agreement)** from the water authority. *Sydney Water.*
- **5.4 (Medium)** VIC: EPA Victoria treats contaminated wash water as industrial waste — capture/contain, keep out of stormwater, general environmental duty applies. *EPA Victoria Pub 980.1.*
- **5.5 (Medium)** Polluting waters is an offence with significant penalties (e.g., NSW POEO Act 1997). *EPA NSW.* — penalty **figures are illustrative/jurisdiction-specific**.

### 6. Carpet cleaning standard & methods (IICRC S100)
- **6.1 (High)** **ANSI/IICRC S100** is the recognised standard for professional cleaning of textile floor coverings; current **S100-2021**. *IICRC / ANSI.*
- **6.2 (Medium)** S100 covers chemistry, fibres/construction, soil management, equipment, methods, safety, pre/post inspection; accommodates **multiple methods**. *ANSI / IICRC.*
- **6.3 (Medium)** **Hot water extraction (HWE)** = deeper soil removal; **encapsulation** = low-moisture, faster return-to-service for occupied facilities — a **dry-time vs soil-load trade-off**. *CMM.*
- ⚠️ IICRC is an **industry certification body, not a government regulator** — do not present S100 as legally mandatory. **Do not state fixed dry times.**

### 7. Vinyl / resilient floor strip & seal
- **7.1 (Medium)** Generic process: **strip → rinse/neutralise → dry → multiple thin sealer/finish coats** with drying between coats. *In-Tec / Tile Cleaners.*
- **7.2 (Medium)** Strippers are typically **alkaline/caustic** — ventilation + PPE required; wet stripped/recoated floor is a **slip/access hazard** while wet/curing (isolate & sign). *SWA WHS duties + process guidance.*
- **7.3 (Medium)** Slip resistance can change after sealing — verify finished floor against target class **per AS 4663**. *Standards Australia.*
- ⚠️ Dilution ratios, coat counts, dry-between-coat & full-cure & re-entry times **must come from the product's SDS/TDS** — flag as product-dependent.

## Guardrails (no overclaiming — mirrors `src/lib/brand-video-assistant.ts`)
- Do **not** claim IICRC/insurer/regulator authority. This is training that **supports** judgement; it does not replace standards, SDS/TDS, qualified professionals, or a site's own rules.
- Do **not** state fixed cure/re-entry/dry times or specific P-rating requirements — defer to SDS/TDS and HB 198/NCC.
- Penalty figures are **illustrative**; verify against current Acts.
- Present WWC checks and contract requirements as **starting points**, not a complete compliance list.

## Unverified / excluded (do NOT assert)
- Specific cure / re-entry / "safe to walk on" times for sealers/finishes → product-specific (SDS/TDS).
- Specific minimum **P-rating** values for particular school/childcare area types → quote HB 198 / NCC directly first.
- Any implication that AS 4586/4663 set re-entry times → they classify/measure only.
- Exact dollar **penalty** figures for stormwater/water-pollution offences → illustrative; verify per current Act.
- Specific NSW/VIC school strip-&-seal **frequencies** → directional; verify against the current contract spec.
- Verbatim **S100** clause wording → standard is paywalled; paraphrase only.
- That a WWC check is the **only** screening a site needs → sites may add child-safe induction.

## Method / reliability note
WebFetch was 403-blocked on most `.gov.au` / Standards / ANSI domains (proxy bot-blocking), so primary-text **verbatim** quoting wasn't captured. **High**-rated claims were each confirmed via the authoritative regulator's own page title/summary and corroborated by ≥1 further source; **Medium** rests on companion guidance/industry restatement. Before publishing any **directly quoted** clause, a human should open the cited `.gov.au`/Standards URL to capture exact wording.
